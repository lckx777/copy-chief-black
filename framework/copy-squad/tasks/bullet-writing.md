## Bullet Formula

```
[CURIOSITY HOOK] + [SPECIFIC BENEFIT] + [PAGE/DETAIL REFERENCE]
```

## 7 Bullet Types

| Type | Structure | Example |
|------|-----------|---------|
| **Curiosity** | "The [specific thing] that [surprising result]" | "The $3 ingredient that dissolves trapped fat faster than $200 diet pills" |
| **Contrarian** | "Why [common belief] is [wrong] -- and what to do instead" | "Why everything your doctor said about cholesterol is potentially dangerous" |
| **Story** | "How [specific person] [achieved specific result]" | "How a retired Navy SEAL taught his wife a 30-second trick for knee pain" |
| **Warning** | "The [specific danger] that [consequence]" | "The common breakfast food that feeds tinnitus crystals (most people eat it daily)" |
| **Secret** | "The [insider thing] that [insiders] use for [result]" | "The 4AM protocol that Brazilian gold medalists swear by" |
| **Number** | "[Exact number] ways to [benefit]" | "7 words that make any bank approve your loan (page 34)" |
| **Question** | "What [unexpected thing] reveals about [their situation]" | "What your tongue color reveals about your liver in the next 5 seconds" |

## Rules

1. Each bullet = MINI-AD (could justify buying on its own)
2. Start with the most intriguing element
3. Include specific number, name, or detail
4. Reference page number for credibility
5. Vary the angle across bullets (no two same type in a row)

## Quality Checklist

- [ ] Each bullet creates standalone desire
- [ ] Mix of 3+ bullet types used
- [ ] Specific numbers in every bullet (non-round)
- [ ] No two consecutive bullets use same structure
- [ ] Page/detail references included
- [ ] Zero generic bullets ("Includes chapter on...")
- [ ] Logo Test: competitor cannot steal any bullet

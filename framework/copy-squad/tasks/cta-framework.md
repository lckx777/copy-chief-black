## CTA Stacking (Kennedy)

```
CTA #1: WHAT TO DO
  "Click the [color] button below right now."

CTA #2: HOW TO DO IT
  "On the next page, fill in your name and email.
   Select your preferred package. That's it -- takes 30 seconds."

CTA #3: WHAT HAPPENS WHEN YOU DO
  "Within 60 seconds, you'll receive [specific thing].
   By tonight, you'll already be [specific first result]."

CTA #4: CONSEQUENCE OF NOT DOING
  "If you leave this page, this offer disappears.
   The price goes to $[higher amount] and [bonus] is gone forever."
```

## CTA Button Text (Not "Submit")

| Weak | Strong |
|------|--------|
| "Submit" | "Send Me [Product] Now" |
| "Buy Now" | "Start My [Transformation] Today" |
| "Learn More" | "Show Me The [Mechanism] Method" |
| "Get Started" | "Claim My [Named Package] Before [Deadline]" |
| "Order" | "Yes, I Want [Specific Result] in [Timeframe]" |

## CTA Placement Rules

```
1. FIRST CTA: After the mechanism reveal (for impatient buyers)
2. SECOND CTA: After the proof section
3. THIRD CTA: After the guarantee (primary conversion point)
4. FOURTH CTA: End of page with urgency wrapper
5. PS CTA: In the PS (Halbert -- most-read section after headline)
```

## PS Template (Halbert)

```
P.S. [Strongest benefit restated] + [Deadline reminder]

Example:
"P.S. Remember, the [Guarantee Name] means you have [X days] to try
[Product] completely risk-free. But the [Bonus Name] disappears at
midnight on [Date]. Click here now before it's gone."
```

## Quality Checklist

- [ ] Minimum 3 CTA instances on page
- [ ] CTA stacking present (WHAT + HOW + WHAT HAPPENS + CONSEQUENCE)
- [ ] Button text is specific (not "Submit" or "Buy Now")
- [ ] Imperative verbs used: "Click", "Call", "Enter", "Claim"
- [ ] Process described step-by-step
- [ ] Immediate aftermath painted (next 60 seconds)
- [ ] Negative CTA present (what they LOSE)
- [ ] PS includes strongest benefit + urgency
- [ ] CTA placed after mechanism, proof, guarantee, and end-of-page

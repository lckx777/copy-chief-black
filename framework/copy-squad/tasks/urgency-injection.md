# Task: Urgency Injection (Without Marketing Speak)

> **Primary Expert:** Dan Kennedy (deadline psychology)
> **Supporting:** Robert Cialdini (scarcity), Joe Sugarman (buying triggers)

---

## Process

### Step 1: Identify Natural Urgency Sources

| Type | Natural? | Specific Detail |
|------|----------|----------------|
| Problem getting worse | YES/NO | "Every day without ___" |
| Seasonal/event-based | YES/NO | "[Specific date/event]" |
| Limited capacity | YES/NO | "[Reason for limit]" |
| Price increase | YES/NO | "[Reason for increase]" |
| Competitor awareness | YES/NO | "Before [industry] catches on" |

### Step 2: Write Urgency Without Banned Phrases

**BANNED:**
- "Act now!" / "Don't wait!"
- "Limited time offer!"
- "Only X spots left!" (without real limit)
- "This won't last forever!"

**USE INSTEAD:**
- Consequence of delay (specific)
- Opportunity cost (quantified)
- Natural deadline (real event)
- Progress compound effect (math)

### Step 3: Stack Urgency (Light → Heavy)
1. Logical: "Each day costs you [specific amount]"
2. Emotional: "[Negative future pace - 30 days]"
3. Social: "[X] people started today, you're still ___"
4. Identity: "The question is: are you someone who ___?"

## Quality Checklist

- [ ] Zero banned phrases used
- [ ] Urgency is NATURAL (not manufactured)
- [ ] Consequence of delay is specific and quantified
- [ ] Stacked from logical to identity
- [ ] Doesn't feel manipulative reading aloud

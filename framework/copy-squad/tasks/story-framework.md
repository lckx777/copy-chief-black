# Task: Story Framework

> **Primary Expert:** Joe Sugarman (flow), Andre Chaperon (narrative arc)
> **Supporting:** Gary Halbert (specificity), Robert Collier (reader entry)

---

## Story Architecture (3-Act)

```
ACT 1: THE WORLD BEFORE (20% of story)
  - Enter reader's current reality (Collier)
  - Establish the protagonist (relatable, specific)
  - Paint the problem with sensory detail (Halbert)
  - Emotional resonance: reader sees THEMSELVES

ACT 2: THE DISCOVERY (50% of story)
  - Inciting incident: what changed everything
  - The struggle: failed attempts (validates reader's experience)
  - The revelation: mechanism discovery
  - Rising action: first signs of change
  - Curiosity seeds throughout (Sugarman)

ACT 3: THE TRANSFORMATION (30% of story)
  - The result: specific, measurable, sensory
  - The new reality: contrast with Act 1
  - The implication: what this means for the READER
  - The bridge: how they can experience the same
```

## Quality Checklist

- [ ] Story follows 3-act structure
- [ ] Protagonist is named with 3+ specific details
- [ ] Minimum 3 sensory details per act
- [ ] Reader identifies with protagonist by sentence 3
- [ ] Mechanism is embedded in the story (not a separate section)
- [ ] Transformation is specific and measurable
- [ ] Third-party reaction included (spouse, doctor, friend)
- [ ] Story creates desire for the product naturally
- [ ] No exit ramps (Sugarman slippery slope throughout)

# Task: Sensory Scene Builder

> **Primary Expert:** Gary Halbert (visceral scenes)
> **Supporting:** Joseph Sugarman (slippery slope), Andre Chaperon (soap opera)

---

## Process

### Step 1: Identify the Emotional Peak
- DRE of this section: ___
- Escalation level target: ___ (4-5 for conversion)
- Time of day: ___ (3AM is more visceral than "nighttime")

### Step 2: Build 5-Sense Layer

| Sense | Generic (REJECT) | Specific (USE) |
|-------|-------------------|----------------|
| Visual | "She felt bad" | "Staring at the bathroom ceiling at 3AM" |
| Auditory | "Heard a noise" | "The buzzing that drowns out your granddaughter's voice" |
| Tactile | "Felt pain" | "That cold sweat soaking through your pillow" |
| Olfactory | "Smelled something" | "The medicinal smell of the 4th prescription this month" |
| Gustatory | "Tasted bad" | "That metallic taste that won't go away since the medication" |

### Step 3: Add Third-Party Reaction
- Who witnesses the scene: ___
- Their exact words: "___"
- Their body language: ___

### Step 4: Anchor to Time + Place
- Exact time: ___ (non-round: 3:17AM > "late at night")
- Exact place: ___ (bathroom > "at home")
- What they were doing: ___ (specific action)

### Step 5: Connect to DRE Escalation
- Level 1-2 (surface): Physical sensation only
- Level 3 (consequence): What this MEANS for their future
- Level 4-5 (relational/identity): How this defines WHO they are

## Quality Checklist

- [ ] Minimum 3/5 senses present
- [ ] Third-party reaction included
- [ ] Non-round time (3:17AM, not 3AM)
- [ ] Specific place (not generic "at home")
- [ ] DRE escalation reaches level 4+
- [ ] Logo Test: Could competitor use this scene? NO
- [ ] Scene makes reader physically FEEL something

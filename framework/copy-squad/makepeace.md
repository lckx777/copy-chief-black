## Voice Patterns

### 1. The Emotional Selling Proposition (ESP)

Every business knows it needs a USP -- a Unique Selling Proposition.
But Makepeace taught that every piece of COPY needs an ESP -- an
Emotional Selling Proposition. The USP tells the brain WHY to buy.
The ESP tells the GUT to buy NOW.

The ESP is not a positioning statement. It is the dominant emotional
thread running through every line of the sales letter. It is the
answer to the question: "What does the prospect FEEL right now about
this problem, and how does buying THIS product make that feeling
go away?"

**The ESP Construction Process:**

Step 1: Identify the prospect's Dominant Resident Emotion (DRE) about the topic.
Step 2: Determine how your product's primary benefit ADDRESSES that emotion.
Step 3: Frame EVERY claim, EVERY bullet, EVERY proof element through the lens of that emotion.
Step 4: Escalate the emotion throughout the copy until NOT buying feels unbearable.
Step 5: Position the purchase as EMOTIONAL RELIEF -- the only way to make the feeling stop.

**The relationship between USP and ESP:**
```
USP = "This supplement contains 4 patented ingredients that dissolve
       calcium deposits in your inner ear."
       (Brain says: "Interesting.")

ESP = "In 14 days, you could wake up to SILENCE for the first time
       in 7 years. No ringing. No buzzing. No lying awake at 3AM
       wondering if this is the sound you'll hear for the rest of
       your life."
       (Gut says: "Give me that. NOW.")
```

The USP is the skeleton. The ESP is the blood, muscle, and nerve
endings. Without the skeleton, the body collapses. Without the blood,
the skeleton is dead.

**DO:**
- Identify the ONE dominant emotion BEFORE writing a single word
- Make the ESP explicit in your research brief -- name the emotion
- Thread the ESP through headline, lead, body, offer, close, and PS
- Use emotional trigger words (from Makepeace's 1,336-word inventory) consistently
- Frame benefits as emotional RELIEF, not intellectual understanding
- Test the ESP with the question: "Does this make the reader FEEL or just THINK?"
- Build the ESP from the prospect's actual language (VOC), not your assumptions

**DON'T:**
- Treat USP and ESP as the same thing (the USP without ESP is dead copy)
- Try to trigger multiple emotions equally (one DOMINANT, others supporting)
- Let the ESP go dormant in the middle of the letter (it must escalate)
- Assume the emotion is always fear (it could be greed, shame, anger, hope)
- Write the ESP in corporate language ("We provide peace of mind" -- dead)
- Let features creep in without emotional framing

### 2. The Power Lead

The first 500 words determine whether the prospect reads or dies.
A power lead grabs them by the emotional collar and REFUSES to let go.
No warm-up. No throat-clearing. IMPACT from word one. Makepeace
believed that if you haven't SEIZED the reader within the first two
sentences, you have lost them permanently.

Makepeace identified six distinct lead types, each calibrated for
a specific audience awareness level and market sophistication stage.
The wrong lead type for the wrong audience is like firing a cannon
at a target behind you.

**Makepeace's 6 Lead Types:**

| # | Type | Structure | When to Use | Energy Level |
|---|------|-----------|-------------|--------------|
| 1 | **The Shock Lead** | Open with a disturbing fact, statistic, or revelation | Problem-aware audience, health/financial markets | 10/10 |
| 2 | **The Story Lead** | Drop reader into the middle of a vivid scene -- in medias res | Unaware/low sophistication, any market | 8/10 |
| 3 | **The Prophecy Lead** | Predict a terrifying or thrilling future event | Financial copy, political, market-crash scenarios | 9/10 |
| 4 | **The Question Lead** | Ask something they cannot NOT answer, targeting their secret pain | Universal frustration, high sophistication | 7/10 |
| 5 | **The Contrast Lead** | Two worlds -- theirs now (hell) vs. theirs after (heaven) | Solution-aware audience, transformation offers | 8/10 |
| 6 | **The Secret Lead** | Reveal that something hidden has been discovered, and the reader is about to learn it | Any awareness level, curiosity-dominant markets | 9/10 |

**The Lead Selection Matrix:**

| Awareness Level | Best Lead Types | Why |
|----------------|----------------|-----|
| Unaware | Story, Secret | Must enter through narrative, not claims |
| Problem-Aware | Shock, Question | Prospect already feels the pain -- amplify it |
| Solution-Aware | Contrast, Prophecy | Prospect has tried solutions -- show THIS one is different |
| Product-Aware | Shock, Prophecy | Must reframe what they think they know |
| Most Aware | Question, Contrast | Short lead, straight to offer |

**Power Lead Rules (NON-NEGOTIABLE):**

1. First sentence must create emotional IMPACT (not information)
2. Build emotional momentum with each successive sentence
3. Use sensory language: sounds, sights, physical sensations
4. Make the reader's current pain VIVID before offering relief
5. Write 5 different leads using 5 different types -- pick the one with most energy
6. NEVER resolve the emotional tension in the lead (keep it OPEN)
7. The lead must contain a PROMISE -- implicit or explicit -- of what the reader will gain

**DO:**
- Write 5 different leads, pick the one with most raw energy
- Open with the most EMOTIONALLY CHARGED element
- Use the prospect's own language from VOC research
- Include a specific number, name, or detail in the first 3 sentences
- Create an information gap that can only be closed by reading further
- Let the lead BREATHE -- short paragraphs, white space, momentum

**DON'T:**
- Start with credentials or product introduction
- Open gently and build slowly (you lose them at gentle)
- Use abstract language in the lead (concrete only)
- Let the lead resolve emotionally (keep tension OPEN)
- Front-load with "Dear Friend" preambles
- Use more than 500 words before the reader knows what the letter is about

### 3. Emotion-Meets-Proof Architecture

The Makepeace sales letter operates on a WAVE pattern -- emotion
and proof alternating in escalating cycles. Never let the reader
coast on emotion alone or drown in proof alone. The brain needs
BOTH. Emotion without proof creates hype-resistance. Proof without
emotion creates boredom. The alternation creates MOMENTUM.

**The Wave Pattern (Canonical Structure):**
```
WAVE 1:
  EMOTION: Vivid scenario of the problem (3-4 sentences)
  PROOF:   Specific statistic or study (1-2 sentences)

WAVE 2:
  EMOTION: What this means for THEM specifically (2-3 sentences)
  PROOF:   Expert quote or testimonial (1-2 sentences)

WAVE 3:
  EMOTION: Escalate -- what happens if they do NOTHING (3-4 sentences)
  PROOF:   Mechanism explanation with specifics (2-3 sentences)

WAVE 4:
  EMOTION: Paint the transformed future -- the relief, the joy (3-4 sentences)
  PROOF:   Case study or before/after with numbers (2-3 sentences)

WAVE 5:
  EMOTION: Urgency -- why NOW, not tomorrow (2-3 sentences)
  PROOF:   Scarcity/authority anchor (1-2 sentences)
```

**The 60/40 Rule:**
Across the entire sales letter, the ratio should be approximately
60% emotional content to 40% proof content. This is NOT a suggestion.
In Makepeace's testing, promos that drifted toward 50/50 lost energy.
Promos that drifted toward 80/20 emotion triggered hype-resistance.
The 60/40 sweet spot is where maximum conversion lives.

**DO:**
- Alternate emotion and proof in waves throughout the copy
- Use emotion to FRAME what the proof means
- Use proof to VALIDATE what the emotion claims
- Escalate emotional intensity with each wave
- Make proof EMOTIONALLY CHARGED (not dry data)
- Pair every major claim with its proof within 2-3 sentences

**DON'T:**
- Stack all emotion in one section and all proof in another
- Use proof that is emotionally neutral (proof should amplify emotion)
- Let emotion run unchecked without proof anchoring (becomes hype)
- Make proof sections longer than emotion sections (60/40 rule)
- Present proof in academic format (cite conversationally, not formally)
- Leave ANY major claim unproven for more than one paragraph

### 4. Makepeace's 4-Legged Stool of Proof

Every sales letter must rest on FOUR legs of proof. Remove any leg
and the stool wobbles. Remove two and the sale collapses. Makepeace
insisted that a promotion with only one or two proof types was
structurally unsound -- it might convert some, but it would never
become a control.

**The 4 Legs:**

| Leg | Type | What It Does | Example |
|-----|------|-------------|---------|
| 1 | **Expert/Authority Proof** | Borrowed credibility from recognized institutions, doctors, researchers | "Dr. Klaus Richter, 17 years at the University of Heidelberg" |
| 2 | **Testimonial/Social Proof** | Real people with real names reporting real results | "Maria G., 64, from Goiania: 'The ringing stopped on day 11'" |
| 3 | **Mechanism/Scientific Proof** | Explaining WHY it works at a biological/technical level | "CoQ10 dissolves the calcium micro-crystals that crush cochlear hair cells" |
| 4 | **Demonstration/Specificity Proof** | Numbers, data, before/after, case studies | "87.3% of 2,847 participants reported significant relief within 14 days" |

**Stool Stability Rules:**
- ALL 4 legs present = control-grade copy
- 3 legs present = publishable but vulnerable to controls
- 2 legs present = weak -- will lose to any competent competitor
- 1 leg present = amateur hour -- do not publish

**Proof Stacking Sequence (Makepeace Order):**
```
1. Mechanism proof FIRST (explains WHY -- creates framework for believing)
2. Expert proof SECOND (validates the mechanism with borrowed authority)
3. Demonstration proof THIRD (shows the mechanism WORKING in numbers)
4. Testimonial proof FOURTH (real people confirm what the numbers promise)
```

This sequence mirrors how the brain processes new information: "How does
it work?" -> "Who says so?" -> "What are the results?" -> "Who has done it?"

### 5. Selling the Diagnosis

In health copy, Makepeace understood that you do NOT sell the cure.
You sell the DIAGNOSIS. The prospect already knows they have a problem
-- the ringing, the pain, the weight, the fatigue. What they DON'T
know is WHY. And that gap between "I have this problem" and "I know
WHY I have this problem" is where the entire sale happens.

When you give the prospect a NEW EXPLANATION for their problem -- a
diagnosis they have never heard before -- you create a paradigm shift.
Everything they thought they knew gets reframed. And the only solution
that makes sense within this NEW framework is YOUR product.

**The Diagnosis Selling Sequence:**

```
Step 1: VALIDATE the problem ("You've been suffering from X")
Step 2: INVALIDATE previous solutions ("Doctors told you Y, but...")
Step 3: REVEAL the real cause ("The REAL reason is Z")
Step 4: NAME the cause ("Scientists call this [MECHANISM NAME]")
Step 5: EXPLAIN why only YOUR solution addresses THIS cause
Step 6: PROVE the mechanism works (4-Legged Stool)
```

**Why this works:**
The diagnosis does three critical things simultaneously:
1. It VALIDATES the prospect's suffering (they're not crazy, not lazy)
2. It EXONERATES them from blame (it's not their fault -- it's THIS mechanism)
3. It POSITIONS your product as the ONLY logical solution within the new framework

**DO:**
- Spend 40-60% of health copy on the PROBLEM and its REAL cause
- Name the mechanism with a proprietary, memorable term
- Use analogies to make complex biology feel simple and visceral
- Show WHY previous solutions failed (they targeted the wrong cause)
- Create an "aha moment" where the reader says "THAT'S why nothing worked!"

**DON'T:**
- Rush to the solution before fully selling the diagnosis
- Use medical jargon without translating to kitchen-table language
- Make the diagnosis sound like a textbook entry (it must be a REVELATION)
- Skip the exoneration step (the reader NEEDS to hear "it's not your fault")
- Present the diagnosis without proof (mechanism proof is non-negotiable)

### 6. Fascination Stacking and the 21 Bullet Types

Fascinations are the engine of direct response copy. They create
DESIRE that cannot be satisfied without buying. A single fascination
strong enough can sell the entire product. A STACK of fascinations
makes it psychologically impossible to say no.

Makepeace identified 21 distinct fascination types in his legendary
"21 Bullet Secrets" newsletter. Each type activates a different
psychological lever. The power of a fascination section comes from
VARIETY -- using 8-12 different types in sequence, never letting
the reader settle into a predictable pattern.

**Makepeace's 21 Fascination Bullet Types:**

| # | Type | Structure | Psychological Lever |
|---|------|-----------|-------------------|
| 1 | **The Naked Benefit** | Direct statement of what they GET | Desire activation |
| 2 | **The Transactional** | "Give me [small action] and I'll give you [big benefit]" | Fair exchange frame |
| 3 | **The Inverted** | "Don't do X until you know Y" | Fear of mistake |
| 4 | **The Curiosity Teaser** | Hints at something extraordinary but withholds the key | Information gap |
| 5 | **The If-Then** | "If you X, then you can Y" | Conditional promise |
| 6 | **The Truth About** | "The truth about X that nobody tells you" | Insider knowledge |
| 7 | **The Why** | "Why X causes Y (and what to do about it)" | Causal curiosity |
| 8 | **The What** | "What X reveals about Y" | Insight promise |
| 9 | **The What Never** | "What you should NEVER do when X" | Danger avoidance |
| 10 | **The Number** | "7 ways to X" / "The 3 foods that Y" | Specificity + completeness |
| 11 | **The Right/Wrong** | "Right and wrong ways to X" | Self-assessment curiosity |
| 12 | **The How To** | "How to X without Y" | Solution without sacrifice |
| 13 | **The Secret** | "The secret X that Y discovered" | Exclusivity + curiosity |
| 14 | **The Warning** | "WARNING: If you X, you must know Y" | Fear of hidden danger |
| 15 | **The Quickest/Easiest** | "The quickest way to X" | Effort minimization |
| 16 | **The Specific Mechanism** | Names a precise method, ingredient, or tool | Credibility via detail |
| 17 | **The Contrarian** | "Why X is actually GOOD for you" | Pattern interrupt |
| 18 | **The Insider** | "What [authority figures] do privately that they'd never tell you" | Conspiracy/exclusivity |
| 19 | **The Time-Bound** | "How to X in [specific timeframe]" | Speed promise |
| 20 | **The Comparison** | "Why X works 3x better than Y" | Competitive superiority |
| 21 | **The PLUS** | "PLUS -- [additional greed-stoking benefit]" | More-more-MORE psychology |

**Fascination Stack Rules:**
- Minimum 15 fascinations per long-form sales letter
- Use AT LEAST 8 different types per stack
- Strongest fascination FIRST (primacy effect)
- Second-strongest LAST (recency effect)
- No two consecutive fascinations of the same type
- Each fascination must stand alone as a selling argument
- PLUS bullet always goes LAST in the stack

**The Gold Standard Test:**
"Would someone buy the ENTIRE product just to learn the answer to
THIS ONE fascination?" If yes, it's a Makepeace-grade fascination.
If no, rewrite or cut.

**DO:**
- Attack each piece of information from 2-3 different angles using different bullet types
- Include at least one specific number per fascination
- Create an information gap that DEMANDS closure
- Write 3x more fascinations than you need, then cut to the best
- Use fascinations in headlines, sub-heads, email subject lines -- not just bullet sections
- Mix formats: some as bullets, some as sub-headlines, some embedded in body copy

**DON'T:**
- Write all fascinations as the same type (monotony kills desire)
- Reveal the answer in the fascination itself (the gap IS the sale)
- Use feature language ("Chapter 3 covers..." -- this is a TABLE OF CONTENTS, not a fascination)
- Write fascinations longer than 3 lines (tight, punchy, compressed)
- Neglect the PLUS bullet at the end (it triggers the greed cascade)
- Stack more than 3 fascinations without a paragraph break (visual overwhelm)

### 7. Dimensionalization

Every benefit in the copy must be DIMENSIONALIZED -- expanded from
a flat claim into a vivid, multi-sensory experience the prospect
can see, hear, feel, and taste. Makepeace's Dimensionalized Benefits
Technique is what separates A-level copy from everything below.

The process is deceptively simple: take any benefit and keep asking
"And what will that do for me?" until you hit the EMOTIONAL bedrock
underneath the rational claim.

**The Dimensionalization Chain:**

```
FEATURE:  Contains CoQ10.
           "And what will that do for me?"
BENEFIT:  Supports cellular energy production.
           "And what will that do for me?"
DEEPER:   You'll have more energy throughout the day.
           "And what will that do for me?"
DEEPER:   You'll stop crashing at 2PM and needing a third cup of coffee.
           "And what will that do for me?"
EMOTIONAL You'll be the parent who runs around with your grandkids at
BEDROCK:  the park instead of watching from the bench. You'll hear them
           laughing and you'll be laughing WITH them -- not catching your
           breath on the sideline.
```

**The 4 Dimensions (ALL must be present):**

| Dimension | What It Does | Example |
|-----------|-------------|---------|
| **Physical** | What the body FEELS | "Wake up without the fog. Your joints don't creak. Your first step out of bed doesn't hurt." |
| **Social** | How OTHERS see them | "Your spouse notices. Your coworkers ask what changed. The barista remembers your name because you're smiling for once." |
| **Emotional** | What the HEART feels | "That knot in your chest -- the one you've carried so long you forgot it was there -- finally loosens." |
| **Identity** | Who they BECOME | "You're not 'the tired one' anymore. You're the one people look at and say, 'What's your secret?'" |

**DO:**
- Dimensionalize the TOP 5 benefits to full depth (all 4 dimensions)
- Use sensory details: sounds, textures, temperatures, smells
- Connect every dimensionalized benefit to a strong resident emotion
- Compare dimensionalized benefits with competitor's FLAT claims
- Use the prospect's actual VOC language for the emotional bedrock

**DON'T:**
- Stop at the rational benefit level ("more energy" -- so what?)
- Use all four dimensions on EVERY benefit (top 5 only -- the rest get 1-2)
- Dimensionalize with generic scenes (must be SPECIFIC to this avatar)
- Skip the "And what will that do for me?" chain -- it reveals the real sale

### 8. The False Closer

The classic "But wait... there's MORE!" technique, mastered by
Makepeace across health and financial promos. Just when the prospect
thinks the offer is complete, you add ANOTHER layer of value.

The psychology: the prospect has been building a mental "should I buy?"
case throughout the letter. The false closer tips the scale by adding
unexpected value at the exact moment of maximum internal deliberation.

**The False Closer Architecture:**
```
1. Present the full offer with price
2. Summarize value ("So you get X, Y, Z -- all for just $49")
3. PAUSE -- let the prospect feel the weight of the offer
4. "But I'm not done..."
5. Add Bonus #1 (unexpected, high-perceived-value)
6. "And there's more..."
7. Add Bonus #2 (complements the main product)
8. Restate the TOTAL value vs. actual price
9. NOW close.
```

**Rules:**
- Maximum 2-3 false closes per letter (more feels manipulative)
- Each bonus must feel GENUINELY valuable (not padding)
- Each bonus must be presented with its OWN dimensionalized benefit
- The false closer works best AFTER the 4-Legged Stool is fully deployed

### 9. Urgency Architecture

Urgency in Makepeace copy is never a tacked-on afterthought. It is
an ARCHITECTURAL element woven throughout the letter from paragraph
one. The urgency must feel ORGANIC -- a natural consequence of the
situation described, not a manufactured deadline.

**3 Types of Makepeace Urgency:**

| Type | Mechanism | Example |
|------|-----------|---------|
| **Consequence Urgency** | What happens if they DON'T act | "Every day without treatment, 3 more hair cells die. They don't regenerate." |
| **Scarcity Urgency** | Limited supply, limited time, limited access | "We only have 2,000 bottles of the new batch -- and 14,000 people read this letter." |
| **Opportunity Urgency** | A window that will close | "This clinical trial pricing ends when the FDA review completes in March." |

**Urgency Layering (Use ALL 3):**
```
BODY:    Consequence urgency (woven throughout -- the cost of inaction)
OFFER:   Scarcity urgency (limited inventory/bonuses)
CLOSE:   Opportunity urgency (deadline, price increase, access window)
```

| 15 | **False Closer #2** | "And I'm STILL not done" | Unexpected bonus #2 |
| 16 | **Risk Reversal** | Guarantee | Make the guarantee SPECIFIC and VISCERAL |

### F5: The Money Spine Architecture

In financial copy, Makepeace used a backbone structure he called the
"Money Spine" -- a series of escalating profit claims connected by
a single investment thesis. Each vertebra of the spine is a specific
trade, stock pick, or financial win with DATE, AMOUNT, and TIMEFRAME.

**The Money Spine Structure:**
```
THESIS:     "One technical indicator has predicted every major move
             in the last 14 years."

VERTEBRA 1: "On March 14, 2019, it flashed 'buy' on NVDA at $42.
             Four months later: $187. A 345% gain."

VERTEBRA 2: "On September 3, 2020, it signaled 'sell' on TSLA at
             $498 -- two days before the crash to $330."

VERTEBRA 3: "On January 11, 2021, it identified a small biotech
             nobody was watching -- ticker MRNA. It was trading at $104."

CONNECTOR:  "Every single time -- the same indicator. The same pattern."

ESCALATION: "And right now, this same indicator is flashing a signal
             I've never seen in 14 years of tracking it."
```

**Rules:**
- Minimum 3 vertebrae (profit examples) per money spine
- Each vertebra must have DATE + TICKER/NAME + ENTRY + EXIT + GAIN
- The spine must connect ALL examples to a single thesis/method
- ESCALATION at the end: "And right now, it's doing it AGAIN"
- Compliance: always include disclaimers/risk language per regulation

### F7: Health Copy Architecture

Makepeace invented the alternative health direct response market.
His health copy follows a specific architecture optimized for
supplement and health product sales.

**The Health Copy Blueprint:**
```
PHASE 1 — THE ALARM (10-15% of copy)
  "Something terrible is happening inside your body right now."
  [Shock lead + vivid symptom scenario]

PHASE 2 — THE DIAGNOSIS (25-35% of copy)
  "And the REAL reason isn't what you've been told."
  [New mechanism + paradigm shift + enemy identification]

PHASE 3 — THE EXONERATION (5-10% of copy)
  "It's NOT your fault. It's not your genes. It's THIS."
  [Remove guilt + blame external cause]

PHASE 4 — THE SOLUTION (15-20% of copy)
  "There IS a way to address [mechanism] directly."
  [Introduce ingredient/product + authority proof]

PHASE 5 — THE EVIDENCE (15-20% of copy)
  "And the proof is overwhelming."
  [4-Legged Stool fully deployed]

PHASE 6 — THE OFFER (10-15% of copy)
  "Here's how to get started TODAY."
  [Stack, bonuses, guarantee, urgency, close]
```

**Health Copy Specific Rules:**
- ALWAYS name the mechanism with a proprietary term
- ALWAYS use an analogy to explain complex biology ("It's like rust forming on a pipe")
- ALWAYS include an enemy (Big Pharma, conventional medicine, the food industry)
- ALWAYS exonerate the prospect before introducing the solution
- NEVER use medical jargon without immediately translating to plain language
- NEVER skip the mechanism -- going straight from "you have a problem" to "buy this pill" is amateur copy

## Anti-Patterns

| # | Anti-Pattern | Why Makepeace Would Reject It | The Fix |
|---|-------------|-------------------------------|---------|
| 1 | **The Intellectual Sell** | "Nobody ever bought a supplement because of a peer-reviewed study alone." Engaging the brain without the gut produces nods, not orders. | Frame EVERY fact through its emotional consequence. "Study X found Y" becomes "Study X means you could wake up without Z for the first time in Y years." |
| 2 | **The Weak Lead** | If the first sentence doesn't create a physical reaction -- a clenched jaw, a racing heart, a leaned-in posture -- the letter is dead. | Write 5 leads. Read each aloud. If none makes you sit up straighter, write 5 more. The lead is the SINGLE most important element. |
| 3 | **The Proof Desert** | A section of 500+ words with zero proof elements. The reader's skeptic brain wakes up and starts whispering "this is bullshit." | Maximum 200 words of emotion before a proof anchor. The wave pattern is not optional. |
| 4 | **Single-Dimension Claims** | "Lose weight" without showing what weight loss MEANS in their life -- their marriage, their mirror, their grandchildren, their identity. | Run every major benefit through the 4-dimension chain: Physical, Social, Emotional, Identity. |
| 5 | **The Feature Dump** | Listing ingredients, specifications, or "what's included" without translating to dimensionalized benefits. | Every feature gets the "And what will that do for me?" chain until you hit emotional bedrock. |
| 6 | **The Monotone Emotion** | Using fear and ONLY fear for 3,000 words. Emotional fatigue sets in at ~500 words of the same emotion. | Rotate through 3-5 emotions. Use the dominant emotion as anchor, secondary emotions as variety. |
| 7 | **The Delayed Start** | Warm-up paragraphs, throat-clearing, "Dear Friend, in today's world..." before any emotional impact. | Cut everything before the first line that creates emotional impact. That IS your opening. |
| 8 | **The Unanchored Claim** | "Thousands of people have found relief." No name. No number. No institution. No timeframe. Ghost claims. | Every claim needs a NAME, a NUMBER, and a PLACE. Minimum. "87% of the 2,847 participants in Dr. Richter's Heidelberg study." |
| 9 | **The Premature Solution** | Introducing the product before fully selling the diagnosis. The prospect hasn't bought the PROBLEM yet. | Spend 35-50% of the letter on the problem/mechanism BEFORE mentioning the solution. |
| 10 | **The Missing Enemy** | No villain. No one to blame. The problem just "exists." This robs the copy of anger, betrayal, and vindication energy. | Every health/financial promo needs an ENEMY: Big Pharma, Wall Street, conventional doctors, the food industry. The enemy gives the prospect permission to feel angry AND to trust YOUR alternative. |
| 11 | **The Flat Fascination** | "Learn about the 5 best vitamins for heart health." This is a table of contents entry, not a fascination. | "The 'forgotten' vitamin that 97% of cardiologists never test for -- even though a $3 test at Walgreens could save your life (page 47)." |
| 12 | **The Orphan Guarantee** | A guarantee buried in small print without dimensionalization. | Makepeace guarantees were EVENTS -- dramatic, specific, named: "Take 90 full days. If the ringing isn't at least 50% quieter... if you aren't sleeping through the night... if your spouse doesn't notice the difference... I'll refund every penny and you KEEP the bottles." |

# Kern Intent-Based Branding -- Master Checklist

> **Source:** Frank Kern Voice DNA Profile
> **Use:** Validate any content, launch, email campaign, or webinar against Kern's IBB principles before delivery.
> **Ref:** `~/.claude/copy-squad/kern.md`

---

## 1. Three Core Messaging Pillars Audit

### Pillar Definition

- [ ] Three distinct pillars are defined for this offer/brand
- [ ] Pillar 1 addresses the MINDSET SHIFT the prospect needs (paradigm change)
- [ ] Pillar 2 addresses the METHOD/MECHANISM they need (how it works)
- [ ] Pillar 3 addresses the PROOF/POSSIBILITY they need (evidence it works for people like them)
- [ ] Each pillar is stated in ONE clear sentence (not vague or compound)
- [ ] Pillars are derived from the GAP between where the prospect IS and where they WANT to be
- [ ] Pillars are mutually exclusive (no overlap or redundancy between them)
- [ ] All three pillars together create a complete journey from skepticism to readiness-to-buy
- [ ] Pillars have been stress-tested: removing any single pillar creates a gap in the sales argument

### Pillar-to-Content Mapping

- [ ] Every piece of content can be mapped to exactly ONE pillar
- [ ] No content exists that does not support a pillar (orphan content = wasted content)
- [ ] Content is distributed roughly equally across all three pillars (no pillar is starved)
- [ ] Content sequence exposes prospects to pillars in logical order (mindset > method > proof)
- [ ] Content within each pillar varies in format (video, email, post, story) to avoid fatigue

## 2. Results-in-Advance Content Audit

### Value Delivery Check

- [ ] Free content solves a SPECIFIC, complete problem (not a teaser or appetizer)
- [ ] Prospect can implement what they learned and get a REAL result from the free content alone
- [ ] Content teaches the HOW, not just the WHAT (actionable steps, not just concepts)
- [ ] The quality of free content exceeds what competitors charge for
- [ ] Content contains frameworks, templates, or step-by-step processes (not just theory)
- [ ] No key information is withheld with "get the rest in my course" gating

### Win Engineering

- [ ] The specific "win" the prospect will experience is identified before content creation
- [ ] The win is achievable within 24-48 hours of consuming the content
- [ ] The win is tangible and measurable (not vague like "you'll feel more confident")
- [ ] The win creates a natural desire for MORE (the paid offer = more of what they just experienced)
- [ ] The "if this is free, what must paid be like?" reaction is the expected outcome

### Generosity Calibration

- [ ] Content GIVES rather than TEASES (giving the recipe, not just showing the dish)
- [ ] Implementation guidance is included (not just information)
- [ ] Case studies within free content demonstrate real results
- [ ] Content could stand alone as a valuable resource even if the offer did not exist
- [ ] The prospect who never buys still benefited from engaging with the content

## 3. Mass Control Phase Verification

### Phase 1: Pre-Launch Seeding

- [ ] Market has been warmed at least 1-2 weeks before PLC 1
- [ ] Curiosity has been built without revealing specific details
- [ ] Social proof signals have been planted (third-party mentions, testimonials, buzz)
- [ ] "Something is coming" energy is present without crossing into hype
- [ ] The audience's attention has been captured and directed toward the upcoming content
- [ ] No premature selling -- this phase is EXCLUSIVELY about anticipation

### Phase 2: PLC Sequence

**PLC 1 -- The Opportunity:**
- [ ] Opens with a compelling vision of what is POSSIBLE
- [ ] Teaches a complete, actionable strategy (not a teaser)
- [ ] Prospect walks away with a usable insight or plan
- [ ] Establishes the BIG IDEA behind the coming offer
- [ ] ZERO product mention -- pure value delivery
- [ ] Ends with curiosity for PLC 2 (open loop)
- [ ] Duration appropriate for depth of content (20-45 min for video)

**PLC 2 -- The Transformation:**
- [ ] Goes deeper into the mechanism/method introduced in PLC 1
- [ ] Includes at least one case study or real-world example
- [ ] Prospect begins to BELIEVE the method could work for them
- [ ] Builds on PLC 1 knowledge (does not repeat, advances)
- [ ] Social proof woven into content (comments, results from PLC 1 implementers)
- [ ] Mechanism is named and framed as proprietary
- [ ] Ends with anticipation for PLC 3

**PLC 3 -- The Experience:**
- [ ] Proof-dominant content: multiple testimonials, demonstrations, live results
- [ ] Prospect sees OTHERS like them succeeding
- [ ] Overcomes "but will it work for ME?" objection through diverse proof
- [ ] Delivers additional value (not just a proof reel)
- [ ] Creates emotional investment in the methodology
- [ ] Prospect is mentally ready to buy before the offer is even presented
- [ ] Seeds the idea that a complete system is available

**PLC 4 -- The Offer:**
- [ ] The offer feels like the NATURAL NEXT STEP, not a surprise pitch
- [ ] Everything taught in PLCs 1-3 is included in the offer (continuity)
- [ ] Value stack is clear and compelling
- [ ] Price is anchored against total value
- [ ] Guarantee removes risk
- [ ] Real deadline established (cart open/close dates)
- [ ] CTA is clear and singular

### Phase 3: Cart Open Period

- [ ] Daily emails during cart open period
- [ ] Each email addresses a DIFFERENT objection (not repetitive pitching)
- [ ] Social proof from early buyers shared in real-time
- [ ] FAQ or "common questions" email included
- [ ] Urgency builds naturally toward the deadline
- [ ] Cart close email sent on final day
- [ ] Tone stays conversational throughout -- never desperate or pushy

### Phase 4: Post-Launch

- [ ] Thank-you communication to buyers
- [ ] Non-buyer sequence continues delivering value (no punishing them for not buying)
- [ ] Results from buyers are collected for future social proof
- [ ] Lessons learned documented for next launch iteration
- [ ] List is segmented based on engagement for future campaigns

## 4. Launch Sequence Architecture

### Email Sequence Check

- [ ] Every email has ONE clear purpose (not multiple competing goals)
- [ ] Subject lines look like personal messages, not promotions
- [ ] Email body is short, punchy, conversational (not long-form sales letters)
- [ ] Each email moves the prospect ONE step forward in the sequence
- [ ] Emails reference previous content naturally ("Did you catch the video I sent Tuesday?")
- [ ] Social proof is woven in conversationally ("John from Ohio just emailed me saying...")
- [ ] CTA is present but not aggressive
- [ ] The "from" field uses a personal name, not a brand name

### Psychological Trigger Sequencing

- [ ] Reciprocity triggered BEFORE asking for anything (free value delivered first)
- [ ] Social proof deployed progressively (increasing quantity and specificity)
- [ ] Commitment/consistency leveraged (small yeses leading to big yes)
- [ ] Scarcity is REAL (actual deadline, actual limits)
- [ ] Authority established through teaching, not claiming
- [ ] Liking built through personality, stories, and relatability

### Anti-Hype Verification

- [ ] No superlatives used without specific backing ("best" → "ranked #1 by [source]")
- [ ] No "revolutionary," "game-changing," "breakthrough," "ultimate" language
- [ ] Effort and work requirements are acknowledged honestly
- [ ] Limitations are stated openly (this is NOT for people who want X)
- [ ] Specific numbers replace vague claims ("$23,800" not "life-changing income")
- [ ] Promises are conservative relative to actual results (underpromise)
- [ ] Disqualification language present ("If you're looking for a magic button, this isn't it")
- [ ] No "fake guru" energy -- no Lamborghini, no mansion tour, no income screenshots without context

### Personality Brand Consistency

- [ ] The person writing this copy has a distinct, identifiable voice
- [ ] The copy could NOT be confused with any competitor's communications
- [ ] Personal stories are specific and detailed (not generic "I struggled too" claims)
- [ ] The writer's personality is the FIRST thing the reader notices
- [ ] Humor, opinions, and tangents are present and feel natural
- [ ] The copy passes the "would I say this to a friend?" test on every sentence

## 6. Intent-Based Branding Content Validation

### Point-Story-Metaphor Structure

For each piece of content:

- [ ] **POINT** is stated clearly in 1-2 sentences (the core idea)
- [ ] **STORY** wraps the point in a narrative (personal experience, client story, or observed event)
- [ ] **METAPHOR** drives the point home with an analogy that makes it sticky and memorable
- [ ] Point, Story, and Metaphor are ALIGNED (they reinforce the same idea, not three different ones)
- [ ] The content supports one of the three Core Messaging Pillars
- [ ] The content delivers a genuine insight or actionable takeaway
- [ ] A prospect who watches/reads this content would describe it as "helpful," not "sales-y"

### Pillar Coverage Audit

- [ ] Pillar 1 (Mindset Shift) has at least 2 content pieces
- [ ] Pillar 2 (Method/Mechanism) has at least 2 content pieces
- [ ] Pillar 3 (Proof/Possibility) has at least 2 content pieces
- [ ] No single pillar has more than 2x the content of any other pillar

## 7. Behavioral Dynamic Response Audit

### Segmentation by Action

- [ ] Engagement behaviors are tracked (video views, clicks, opens, page visits)
- [ ] Prospects are segmented by BEHAVIOR, not just demographics
- [ ] Different engagement levels trigger different follow-up sequences
- [ ] Response indicators are defined and mapped to next actions
- [ ] Non-engagers receive re-engagement content (not the same sequence)

## 8. 4 Day Cash Machine Validation

### Pre-Campaign Check

- [ ] A BELIEVABLE reason for the discount exists (not just "I feel like having a sale")
- [ ] The reason is specific and personal (anniversary, new version coming, testing, capacity)
- [ ] The discount is significant enough to create urgency (40-60% off recommended)
- [ ] Bonuses are prepared to STACK on top of the discount (extra value, not just lower price)
- [ ] The deadline is exactly 4 days -- no flexibility, no extensions

### Day-by-Day Verification

**Day 1 -- Announcement:**
- [ ] Subject line is curiosity-driven, not promotional ("so I made a decision")
- [ ] Reason for discount is explained upfront (builds trust, not suspicion)
- [ ] Offer details are clear: what they get, original price, discounted price
- [ ] Bonuses are introduced
- [ ] 4-day deadline is established
- [ ] Tone is casual -- "here's the deal" not "LIMITED TIME OFFER!!!"
- [ ] ONE clear CTA

**Day 2 -- Reinforcement:**
- [ ] Addresses the #1 objection prospects are likely to have
- [ ] Includes a specific case study or success story
- [ ] Deadline reiterated naturally ("3 more days" or similar)
- [ ] Tone stays conversational -- not more aggressive than Day 1
- [ ] New angle or benefit highlighted (not just repeating Day 1)

**Day 3 -- Intensification:**
- [ ] New bonus or incentive added (creates "act now" energy)
- [ ] Social proof from people who already bought during the campaign
- [ ] Urgency language present but not desperate ("48 hours left")
- [ ] Content still delivers value, not just "buy buy buy"
- [ ] Directly addresses fence-sitters with empathy

**Day 4 -- Final Push:**
- [ ] Subject line signals finality ("last call" / "closing tonight")
- [ ] Clean recap of the complete offer (for anyone who missed previous emails)
- [ ] All bonuses expire tonight -- stated clearly
- [ ] No new arguments -- just summary and CTA
- [ ] "If this isn't for you, no worries" energy (anti-desperation)
- [ ] Specific close time stated (e.g., "midnight Pacific")
- [ ] ZERO hint of possible extension (credibility depends on this)

### Post-Campaign Integrity

- [ ] Cart actually closes at the stated time
- [ ] No "just kidding, I extended it" follow-up email (ever)
- [ ] Non-buyers receive value content within 24 hours (no sulking)
- [ ] Results documented for future campaigns

## 9. Webinar Blueprint Validation

### Pre-Webinar System

- [ ] Registration page promises specific value (not vague "learn about X")
- [ ] Liquidator offer is tested (offer made to registrants BEFORE the webinar)
- [ ] Pre-indoctrination sequence is set up (3-5 communications before the webinar)
- [ ] Pre-indoc sequence builds authority, overcomes skepticism, increases desire
- [ ] Show-up/reminder sequence is set up (day before, morning of, 15 min before)
- [ ] Attendance incentive is in place (bonus for live attendees)

### Six-Part Structure Check

**Part 1 -- Opening Hook (2-5 min):**
- [ ] Pattern interrupt in the first 30 seconds
- [ ] Establishes "this is NOT a normal webinar" expectation
- [ ] Previews what they will learn (creates commitment to stay)
- [ ] Tone is casual, personal, NOT "welcome to our presentation"

**Part 2 -- Authority Build (5-10 min):**
- [ ] Brief credentials (under 3 minutes -- not a resume recitation)
- [ ] STORY of discovery or origin (not a list of achievements)
- [ ] Credentials demonstrated through expertise, not claimed through titles
- [ ] Self-deprecating element present (prevents "guru" perception)

**Part 3 -- Core Teaching (20-30 min):**
- [ ] Three pillars are taught with genuine depth and actionability
- [ ] Prospect could implement and get a result from this section alone
- [ ] Teaching follows Point-Story-Metaphor for each pillar
- [ ] Engagement maintained through stories, examples, and practical exercises
- [ ] NO product mention during this section
- [ ] Content quality exceeds what competitors charge for in paid courses

**Part 4 -- Transition Bridge (2-3 min):**
- [ ] Natural pivot from teaching to offer ("Now, here's the thing...")
- [ ] Acknowledges the gap between what was taught and full implementation
- [ ] Introduces the idea of a complete system without pitching yet
- [ ] Feels like a continuation, not a jarring shift

**Part 5 -- Offer Presentation (10-15 min):**
- [ ] Twelve-Part Offer Formula followed (see below)
- [ ] Value stack is visually clear and compelling
- [ ] Price anchored against total value (at least 10x)
- [ ] Guarantee is strong and clearly stated
- [ ] Scarcity/urgency elements are REAL
- [ ] CTA is singular and clear

**Part 6 -- Q&A / Close (10-20 min):**
- [ ] Objections handled through additional stories and proof
- [ ] Social proof shared in real-time (live chat, live purchases)
- [ ] Final CTA repeated with urgency
- [ ] Graceful close that does not pressure non-buyers

### Twelve-Part Offer Formula

- [ ] Product is named (proprietary name, not generic)
- [ ] Deliverables are listed specifically (what they physically receive)
- [ ] Outcomes are stated for each deliverable (what it DOES for them)
- [ ] Social proof is integrated (testimonials, case studies, numbers)
- [ ] Fast-action bonuses are compelling and time-limited
- [ ] Total value stack is calculated and displayed
- [ ] Price anchor: "You'd expect to pay $X" (based on real comparisons)
- [ ] Actual price reveal: significantly below anchor
- [ ] Guarantee: specific, generous, frictionless (risk reversal)
- [ ] Scarcity: real limit (enrollment cap, bonus expiration, charter price)
- [ ] Urgency: real deadline (not "limited time" -- a specific date/time)
- [ ] CTA: one action, stated clearly, repeated

## 12. Quick Diagnostic (Pre-Flight Check)

Use this 10-item checklist before shipping ANY Kern-style content:

- [ ] Is this content genuinely helpful? Could the prospect profit from it without buying?
- [ ] Does this sound like a PERSON talking, not a marketer writing?
- [ ] Are there specific numbers instead of vague claims?
- [ ] Is the personality of the writer present and distinctive?
- [ ] Does this support one of the three Core Messaging Pillars?
- [ ] Would a competitor be embarrassed to put their name on this? (Logo Test)
- [ ] Is the tone consistent whether teaching or selling?
- [ ] Are there real stories with specific details (names, dates, amounts)?
- [ ] Does the CTA feel like a natural next step, not a jarring pitch?
- [ ] Could I send this email/content to a friend without feeling weird about it?

**If any answer is NO, revise before shipping.**

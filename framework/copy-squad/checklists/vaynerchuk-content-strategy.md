## Section 1: Attention Audit (Where Is the Attention?)

Before creating content, audit where attention exists and whether it is underpriced.

| # | Question | Assessment | Status |
|---|----------|-----------|--------|
| 1 | Where is the target audience spending the most TIME right now? | [ ] Platform usage data reviewed | [ ] |
| 2 | Where is organic reach highest relative to effort? | [ ] Reach-per-post analyzed per platform | [ ] |
| 3 | Where are CPMs lowest relative to attention quality? | [ ] Paid media CPMs compared across platforms | [ ] |
| 4 | Where are competitors NOT yet present or active? | [ ] Competitive gap identified | [ ] |
| 5 | Which platforms recently launched new features with boosted reach? | [ ] New features mapped | [ ] |
| 6 | Is 10-20% of content effort allocated to experimental/emerging channels? | [ ] Emerging channel budget allocated | [ ] |

**Scoring:** 5-6 checked = strong attention strategy. 3-4 = gaps to address. <3 = flying blind.

### Jab Quality Check

Not all jabs are created equal. A weak jab is just noise.

| # | Quality Criterion | Yes? |
|---|-------------------|------|
| 1 | Does the jab deliver standalone value (useful even if they never buy)? | [ ] |
| 2 | Would the audience share this with someone who does not follow you? | [ ] |
| 3 | Is the jab entertaining, educational, OR emotionally resonant? | [ ] |
| 4 | Does the jab use the platform's native language and format? | [ ] |
| 5 | Could this jab work WITHOUT your brand name on it? (It should -- that is what makes it generous) | [ ] |

### Right Hook Quality Check

A right hook without proper setup is a sucker punch, not a sale.

| # | Quality Criterion | Yes? |
|---|-------------------|------|
| 1 | Is the CTA clear, specific, and easy to execute on mobile? | [ ] |
| 2 | Does the right hook match the platform's conventions? | [ ] |
| 3 | Has this ask been EARNED by preceding jabs? | [ ] |
| 4 | Is there a specific reason to act NOW (not generic urgency)? | [ ] |
| 5 | Would you click/buy this if you saw it from a brand you follow? | [ ] |

## Section 9: Anti-Pattern Detection

Check for the most common mistakes Vaynerchuk calls out.

| # | Anti-Pattern | Detected? | Severity | Fix |
|---|-------------|-----------|----------|-----|
| 1 | Content looks like an ad, not like native platform content | [ ] | HIGH | Rewrite as conversation, reduce production value |
| 2 | Same content posted identically across platforms | [ ] | HIGH | Create platform-native versions of each idea |
| 3 | More right hooks than jabs in the last 20 posts | [ ] | HIGH | Reset to 4:1 jab ratio immediately |
| 4 | Comments section is ignored (posting and ghosting) | [ ] | HIGH | Implement $1.80 strategy + respond to all comments |
| 5 | No emerging platform experimentation | [ ] | MEDIUM | Allocate 10-20% effort to newest channel |
| 6 | Content is "safe" -- no polarizing opinions or strong positions | [ ] | MEDIUM | Add opinion-driven content to the mix |
| 7 | Optimizing for follower count instead of engagement | [ ] | MEDIUM | Shift metrics to engagement rate, saves, shares |
| 8 | No behind-the-scenes/documentary content | [ ] | MEDIUM | Start documenting journey 1x/day |
| 9 | Social content requires multiple approval layers (slow) | [ ] | MEDIUM | Streamline to single approver or pre-approved frameworks |
| 10 | No user-generated content amplification | [ ] | LOW | Build UGC program, share audience content weekly |
| 11 | Content calendar planned monthly (not weekly/daily) | [ ] | LOW | Shift to weekly planning with daily cultural additions |
| 12 | No A/B testing of hooks, formats, or CTAs | [ ] | LOW | Test 2-3 variations of every right hook |

**Anti-Pattern Count:**

| Count | Assessment |
|-------|-----------|
| 0-2 detected | Clean operation. Focus on optimization. |
| 3-5 detected | Warning signs. Address HIGH severity items first. |
| 6-8 detected | Significant gaps. Content strategy needs restructuring. |
| 9+ detected | Full overhaul required. Start from Core Philosophy in Voice DNA profile. |

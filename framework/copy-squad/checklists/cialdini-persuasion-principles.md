# Cialdini 7 Principles of Persuasion -- Audit Checklist

> **Expert:** Robert Cialdini
> **Source:** Influence (1984), Pre-Suasion (2016) + Voice DNA Profile
> **Use:** Complete audit for all 7 principles of persuasion plus Pre-Suasion. Run on EVERY piece of copy before shipping. Identifies missing principles, weak deployments, and ethical violations.
> **Cross-ref:** `cialdini.md` Frameworks 1-10 for theory, research, and examples.

---

## Part 1: Reciprocity Audit

### Checkpoints

| # | Checkpoint | Question | Pass/Fail |
|---|-----------|----------|-----------|
| 1.1 | **Value Before Ask** | Does the copy give genuine value (insight, tool, revelation) BEFORE making any request? | [ ] |
| 1.2 | **Unexpected Gift** | Is there at least one moment of UNEXPECTED value that goes beyond what the prospect expected? | [ ] |
| 1.3 | **Personalization** | Is the value personalized to the prospect's specific situation ("for people like you who...") rather than generic? | [ ] |
| 1.4 | **Multiple Gifts** | Are there at least 2-3 distinct value-giving moments throughout the copy (not all front-loaded)? | [ ] |
| 1.5 | **Genuine Value** | Would the prospect find value in what was given even if they NEVER purchased? | [ ] |
| 1.6 | **No Strings Visible** | Is the gift given WITHOUT visible expectation? (No "I'm giving you this so you should buy...") | [ ] |
| 1.7 | **Bonus Stack** | Does the offer include bonuses that function as additional reciprocity triggers? | [ ] |
| 1.8 | **Concession Present** | Is there a concession element? ("I considered charging X, but decided to offer it at Y because...") | [ ] |

**Reciprocity Score: ___ / 8**

| Score | Rating | Action |
|-------|--------|--------|
| 7-8 | STRONG | Reciprocity is well-deployed |
| 5-6 | ADEQUATE | Add 1-2 unexpected value moments |
| 3-4 | WEAK | Restructure opening to lead with value |
| Below 3 | MISSING | Copy asks before giving. Restructure entirely. |

## Part 2: Commitment and Consistency Audit

### Checkpoints

| # | Checkpoint | Question | Pass/Fail |
|---|-----------|----------|-----------|
| 2.1 | **Opening Agreement** | Does the copy start with a statement or question the prospect naturally agrees with? | [ ] |
| 2.2 | **Micro-Commitment Chain** | Are there at least 5 escalating micro-commitments before the purchase ask? | [ ] |
| 2.3 | **Self-Identification** | Does the copy invite the prospect to IDENTIFY as someone specific? ("If you're the kind of person who...") | [ ] |
| 2.4 | **Active Commitment** | Is there at least one moment requiring ACTIVE response? (quiz answer, click, scroll decision) | [ ] |
| 2.5 | **Voluntary Feel** | Does every commitment feel VOLUNTARY? (No forced opt-ins, pre-checked boxes, manipulative UX) | [ ] |
| 2.6 | **Belief Commitment** | Does the prospect agree with the MECHANISM before seeing the offer? (Belief precedes purchase) | [ ] |
| 2.7 | **Consistency Bridge** | Is the purchase framed as the LOGICAL and CONSISTENT next step after all prior agreements? | [ ] |
| 2.8 | **Post-Purchase Reinforcement** | Does post-purchase copy reinforce the decision? ("You made the right choice.") | [ ] |
| 2.9 | **Progressive Escalation** | Do commitments ESCALATE gradually (mental nod -> click -> time -> email -> purchase)? | [ ] |

**Commitment Score: ___ / 9**

| Score | Rating | Action |
|-------|--------|--------|
| 8-9 | STRONG | Commitment chain is well-built |
| 6-7 | ADEQUATE | Add 1-2 intermediate commitments |
| 4-5 | WEAK | Map the full commitment ladder and fill gaps |
| Below 4 | MISSING | No commitment chain exists. Build from scratch. |

## Part 3: Social Proof Audit

### Checkpoints

| # | Checkpoint | Question | Pass/Fail |
|---|-----------|----------|-----------|
| 3.1 | **Numerical Proof** | Are there specific, non-round numbers? ("12,847" not "thousands") | [ ] |
| 3.2 | **Named Testimonials** | Are testimonials from NAMED individuals with specific details (age, city, profession)? | [ ] |
| 3.3 | **Similarity Match** | Do testimonials/proof come from people SIMILAR to the target prospect? (Same demographics, same struggle) | [ ] |
| 3.4 | **Expert Endorsement** | Is there at least one expert/institutional endorsement? | [ ] |
| 3.5 | **Proof Stacking** | Are at least 3 DIFFERENT types of social proof used? (numbers, named, expert, media, real-time) | [ ] |
| 3.6 | **Result Specificity** | Do testimonials include SPECIFIC results? ("Lost 23kg in 87 days" not "lost weight") | [ ] |
| 3.7 | **Mechanism Proof** | Is there proof that the MECHANISM works (not just the product)? (Study, research, expert validation) | [ ] |
| 3.8 | **Recency** | Is the social proof RECENT? (Dates, "this month," current year references) | [ ] |
| 3.9 | **Context Match** | Is the social proof in the RIGHT context? (Proof for the claim it supports, not generic) | [ ] |
| 3.10 | **Cascade Order** | Does proof escalate from broad (numbers) to specific (named story) to expert to real-time? | [ ] |

**Social Proof Score: ___ / 10**

| Score | Rating | Action |
|-------|--------|--------|
| 9-10 | STRONG | Social proof cascade is complete |
| 7-8 | ADEQUATE | Add missing proof types from the stack |
| 5-6 | WEAK | Major gaps. Add similarity-matched proof and specificity. |
| Below 5 | CRITICAL | Insufficient social proof. Build the cascade from scratch. |

## Part 4: Authority Audit

### Checkpoints

| # | Checkpoint | Question | Pass/Fail |
|---|-----------|----------|-----------|
| 4.1 | **Credentials Visible** | Are the expert's credentials stated clearly and EARLY? (Title, institution, years of experience) | [ ] |
| 4.2 | **Institutional Association** | Is the expert associated with a recognized institution? (University, hospital, publication) | [ ] |
| 4.3 | **Demonstrated Knowledge** | Does the expert DEMONSTRATE knowledge through specific insights (not just claim expertise)? | [ ] |
| 4.4 | **Credibility Paradox** | Is there at least one admission of limitation? ("This doesn't work for everyone..." or "12.7% saw no improvement...") | [ ] |
| 4.5 | **Third-Party Validation** | Is the expert's authority validated by a THIRD PARTY? (Journal, media, peer, institution) | [ ] |
| 4.6 | **Insider Knowledge** | Does the expert reveal something only a genuine insider would know? (Not generic information) | [ ] |
| 4.7 | **Authority Consistency** | Is authority reinforced at multiple points? (Introduction, mechanism reveal, CTA) | [ ] |
| 4.8 | **Genuine Credentials** | Are ALL claimed credentials VERIFIABLE and TRUE? (Fabricated authority = instant credibility collapse) | [ ] |

**Authority Score: ___ / 8**

| Score | Rating | Action |
|-------|--------|--------|
| 7-8 | STRONG | Authority is well-established |
| 5-6 | ADEQUATE | Add demonstrated knowledge or third-party validation |
| 3-4 | WEAK | Authority is claimed but not demonstrated. Add proof of expertise. |
| Below 3 | MISSING | No credible authority present. Establish before proceeding. |

## Part 5: Liking Audit

### Checkpoints

| # | Checkpoint | Question | Pass/Fail |
|---|-----------|----------|-----------|
| 5.1 | **Similarity Signal** | Does the copy or expert demonstrate SIMILARITY to the prospect? (Shared background, shared struggle) | [ ] |
| 5.2 | **Empathy Demonstration** | Is there genuine empathy for the prospect's situation? (Not just "I understand" but SPECIFIC understanding) | [ ] |
| 5.3 | **Vulnerability** | Does the expert or narrator share a personal vulnerability or struggle? | [ ] |
| 5.4 | **Cooperation Framing** | Is there a "we're in this together" or "us vs. them" framing? | [ ] |
| 5.5 | **Conversational Tone** | Does the copy feel like a conversation with someone you LIKE, not a pitch from a stranger? | [ ] |
| 5.6 | **Earned Compliments** | If compliments are present, are they EARNED and SPECIFIC? ("You've already shown courage by looking for answers" not "You're amazing!") | [ ] |
| 5.7 | **Story Connection** | Is there a personal story that creates emotional connection with the reader? | [ ] |
| 5.8 | **No Condescension** | Does the copy avoid talking DOWN to the prospect? (Respects their intelligence and past efforts) | [ ] |

**Liking Score: ___ / 8**

| Score | Rating | Action |
|-------|--------|--------|
| 7-8 | STRONG | Liking is well-established |
| 5-6 | ADEQUATE | Add similarity signals or a vulnerability moment |
| 3-4 | WEAK | Copy feels like a pitch, not a conversation. Add empathy and story. |
| Below 3 | MISSING | No liking established. The prospect has no reason to listen. |

## Part 6: Scarcity Audit

### Checkpoints

| # | Checkpoint | Question | Pass/Fail |
|---|-----------|----------|-----------|
| 6.1 | **Real Limitation** | Is the scarcity GENUINE? (Real stock limits, real deadlines, real batch constraints) | [ ] |
| 6.2 | **Explained Reason** | Is there a BELIEVABLE reason for the scarcity? ("Due to sourcing from..." or "This batch was limited to...") | [ ] |
| 6.3 | **Specific Numbers** | Are scarcity numbers SPECIFIC? ("47 kits remaining" not "limited stock") | [ ] |
| 6.4 | **Loss Framing** | Is scarcity framed as LOSS ("what you'll miss") rather than gain ("what you'll get")? | [ ] |
| 6.5 | **Value First** | Has substantial VALUE been established BEFORE scarcity is introduced? (Scarcity amplifies zero = zero) | [ ] |
| 6.6 | **Dual Scarcity** | Are BOTH quantity and time scarcity present? (Combined effect is stronger than either alone) | [ ] |
| 6.7 | **Consequence of Missing** | Is the cost of MISSING the opportunity made concrete and visceral? | [ ] |
| 6.8 | **No Fake Urgency** | Are countdown timers, "only X left" claims, and deadline statements ALL verifiable and real? | [ ] |
| 6.9 | **Scarcity Timing** | Is scarcity introduced in the SECOND HALF of the copy? (After value, proof, and commitment) | [ ] |

**Scarcity Score: ___ / 9**

| Score | Rating | Action |
|-------|--------|--------|
| 8-9 | STRONG | Scarcity is genuine and well-calibrated |
| 6-7 | ADEQUATE | Strengthen the reason or add loss framing |
| 4-5 | WEAK | Scarcity feels artificial. Ground it in reality. |
| Below 4 | CRITICAL | Either missing or fabricated. Fix before shipping. |

## Part 7: Unity Audit

### Checkpoints

| # | Checkpoint | Question | Pass/Fail |
|---|-----------|----------|-----------|
| 7.1 | **Tribal Identification** | Does the copy establish a shared IDENTITY between communicator and prospect? ("We" not "you and I") | [ ] |
| 7.2 | **Shared Enemy** | Is there a common adversary that unites communicator and prospect? | [ ] |
| 7.3 | **"We" Language** | Is "we/us/our" used consistently throughout (not just at the CTA)? | [ ] |
| 7.4 | **Family Framing** | Is there at least one family-level framing? ("What I'd tell my own mother/brother/child...") | [ ] |
| 7.5 | **Shared Experience** | Does the copy reference experiences the prospect and communicator BOTH had? | [ ] |
| 7.6 | **Membership Invitation** | Is the purchase framed as JOINING a group rather than buying a product? | [ ] |
| 7.7 | **Genuine Shared Ground** | Is the shared identity GENUINE? (Not appropriated or fabricated) | [ ] |
| 7.8 | **Advice Not Opinion** | When seeking engagement, does the copy ask for "advice" or "help" rather than "opinion"? | [ ] |

**Unity Score: ___ / 8**

| Score | Rating | Action |
|-------|--------|--------|
| 7-8 | STRONG | Unity is deeply woven into the copy |
| 5-6 | ADEQUATE | Add shared enemy or family framing |
| 3-4 | WEAK | Copy feels like "seller to buyer" not "us together." Add tribal language. |
| Below 3 | MISSING | No unity present. Establish shared identity in hook/problem section. |

## Part 8: Pre-Suasion Audit

### Checkpoints

| # | Checkpoint | Question | Pass/Fail |
|---|-----------|----------|-----------|
| 8.1 | **Pre-Suasive Opening** | Do the first 60 seconds prime the prospect for the message that follows? | [ ] |
| 8.2 | **Self-Identification Question** | Is there a question that activates self-identification BEFORE the pitch? ("Are you someone who...?") | [ ] |
| 8.3 | **Cost-of-Inaction Anchor** | Is the cost of NOT acting established BEFORE the price is revealed? | [ ] |
| 8.4 | **Concept Priming** | Are concepts that support the core message activated BEFORE that message is delivered? | [ ] |
| 8.5 | **Privileged Moment** | Is the actual offer/ask timed for a moment when the prospect is maximally receptive? | [ ] |
| 8.6 | **Environmental Alignment** | Does the page/video design (colors, images, layout) support the message rather than contradict it? | [ ] |
| 8.7 | **Attention Channeling** | Is attention directed to the ONE most important idea before distractions appear? | [ ] |

**Pre-Suasion Score: ___ / 7**

| Score | Rating | Action |
|-------|--------|--------|
| 6-7 | STRONG | Message lands on prepared mental soil |
| 4-5 | ADEQUATE | Strengthen the opening prime |
| 2-3 | WEAK | Opening does not prepare the prospect. Redesign first 60 seconds. |
| Below 2 | MISSING | No pre-suasion present. The message fights against the context. |

## Part 9: Ethical Compliance Audit

### Checkpoints

| # | Checkpoint | Question | Pass/Fail |
|---|-----------|----------|-----------|
| 9.1 | **Mutual Benefit** | Would the prospect GENUINELY benefit from the action being promoted? | [ ] |
| 9.2 | **6-Month Test** | Would the prospect THANK YOU for this influence 6 months from now? | [ ] |
| 9.3 | **No Fabrication** | Is every piece of social proof, authority claim, and scarcity element REAL? | [ ] |
| 9.4 | **Voluntary Compliance** | Could the prospect EASILY walk away at any point? (No dark patterns, no traps) | [ ] |
| 9.5 | **Honest Limitations** | Are the product's limitations acknowledged somewhere? (Credibility paradox) | [ ] |
| 9.6 | **No Exploitation** | Is the copy influencing toward a good decision or exploiting vulnerability? | [ ] |

**Ethics Score: ___ / 6**

| Score | Rating | Action |
|-------|--------|--------|
| 6 | CLEAN | Ethically sound influence |
| 4-5 | REVIEW | Check flagged items for ethical alignment |
| Below 4 | STOP | Ethical violations present. Do NOT ship until resolved. |

## Part 12: Principle Interaction Matrix

Some principles AMPLIFY each other. Others can CONFLICT if deployed
carelessly. Use this matrix to ensure principle combinations work
together rather than against each other.

### Amplifying Combinations (USE THESE)

| Combination | Effect | Example |
|-------------|--------|---------|
| **Authority + Social Proof** | Expert validation + peer validation = maximum credibility | "Dr. X recommends it, and 12,847 people have used it" |
| **Unity + Liking** | Shared identity + personal rapport = deep trust | "As someone who also suffered... we know what this is like" |
| **Reciprocity + Commitment** | Gift creates obligation + first yes leads to more | Free value -> small action -> larger commitment |
| **Scarcity + Social Proof** | Limited + popular = maximum desire | "Only 47 left -- 312 ordered yesterday" |
| **Pre-Suasion + All Others** | Right mental state amplifies every principle | Prime before deploying any principle |
| **Authority + Credibility Paradox** | Admitting weakness + expertise = deep trust | "It doesn't work for 12.7% of people -- here's why..." |

### Conflicting Combinations (AVOID THESE)

| Conflict | Problem | Fix |
|----------|---------|-----|
| **Scarcity + Abundance** | "Only 47 left!" + "Millions have tried it!" sends mixed signals | Choose one framing per section. Scarcity at CTA, abundance in proof. |
| **Authority + Excessive Liking** | Too friendly undermines expertise | Calibrate: warm but professional, not buddy-buddy |
| **Unity (exclusive) + Social Proof (mass)** | "For the elite few" + "Millions trust us" contradicts | Match unity framing to proof framing (exclusive + curated proof) |
| **Multiple Scarcity Types** | Quantity + Time + Price + Bonus scarcity at once = overwhelm | Maximum 2 scarcity types per CTA |

## Part 13: Red Flags (Instant Fail)

If ANY of these are present, the copy FAILS the audit regardless
of total score.

| # | Red Flag | Why | Fix |
|---|----------|-----|-----|
| 1 | Fake countdown timer (resets on refresh) | Permanent trust destruction | Use real deadline or remove |
| 2 | Fabricated testimonials | If discovered, ALL credibility collapses | Replace with real testimonials, even imperfect |
| 3 | False authority claims | Unverifiable credentials backfire | Use demonstrable expertise instead |
| 4 | Scarcity before value | Urgency on nothing = pushy salesman | Establish value FIRST, then introduce scarcity |
| 5 | Zero social proof | No evidence anyone else has benefited | Add at minimum numerical + 1 named testimonial |
| 6 | Forced commitments | Pre-checked boxes, trapped UX | Make all commitments clearly voluntary |
| 7 | No reciprocity (all ask, no give) | Prospect feels exploited, not served | Lead with genuine value before any ask |
| 8 | Ethics score below 4 | Manipulation, not influence | Stop and fix ethical issues before ANY other work |

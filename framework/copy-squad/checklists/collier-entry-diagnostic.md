# Collier Entry Diagnostic Checklist

> **Expert:** Robert Collier
> **Source:** The Robert Collier Letter Book, Voice DNA Profile (collier.md)
> **Use:** Diagnose reader entry point, construct empathy bridge, audit desire channeling, validate specificity, and structure letters. Run BEFORE writing any lead, opening, or empathy bridge section. Minimum: 35/50 checks passed for delivery.

---

## Category 1: Reader Entry Diagnostic (The Conversation)

Before writing a SINGLE word, you must identify the conversation already
happening in the reader's mind. These 5 questions are NON-NEGOTIABLE.
Answer all 5 with specific, documented answers before opening your writing tool.

| # | Check | How to Complete |
|---|-------|-----------------|
| 1 | [ ] **Current Worry Identified** -- You can state in one sentence what the reader is worried about RIGHT NOW (not in general, but at this moment in their life). Written in THEIR words, not yours. | Pull from VOC. Not "they worry about health" but "they're worried the ringing means they're going deaf." The answer must be specific enough that only THIS audience would say it. |
| 2 | [ ] **2 AM Conversation Documented** -- You can describe the conversation the reader has with themselves at 2 AM -- the unspoken fear, the shameful desire, the truth they avoid in daylight. | This is the DEEP conversation. Not the polite version. "What if this never stops?" "What if she leaves?" "What if I fail AGAIN?" Pull from VOC comments with high emotional intensity (4-5). |
| 3 | [ ] **Exact Vocabulary Captured** -- You have 10+ specific phrases the reader uses to describe their problem, pulled directly from VOC research. Not paraphrased. EXACT quotes. | Forum posts, comments, reviews, social media. "This goddamn ringing" not "auditory discomfort." "Nothing works" not "ineffective treatments." The words must be THEIRS. |
| 4 | [ ] **Failed Solutions Listed** -- You can list at least 3 specific things the reader has already tried and failed with, in the order they typically try them. | This maps the reader's journey BEFORE your copy. "First they try ignoring it. Then they see an ENT. Then they try white noise apps. Then hearing aids. Then they Google 'tinnitus cure' at 3 AM." |
| 5 | [ ] **Emotional Temperature Measured** -- You can name the reader's DOMINANT emotion right now AND its intensity level (1-5 on the emotional escalation scale). | Not just "frustrated" but "frustrated at Level 3 (consequence-level): they've been dealing with this for months/years and believe it may never end." The temperature determines your opening's tone. |

**GATE:** If any of these 5 checks fail, STOP. Do not write. Return to VOC research.

## Category 2: Empathy Bridge Construction Guide (4 Steps)

The Empathy Bridge moves the reader from "you understand me" to "you can help me."
Use this fill-in template to construct each step before integrating into copy.

| # | Check | Template |
|---|-------|----------|
| 6 | [ ] **Step 1: MIRROR Written** -- You have written 2-4 sentences that describe the reader's current reality so precisely they think you have been watching them. Uses THEIR words. Present tense. Specific scenarios. | *"You've been to [number] doctors. Maybe [number+1]. Each one [specific action they take]. And each time, you [specific feeling]. Because the [problem] is REAL -- it's there RIGHT NOW -- and [what the doctors say] doesn't [what they wish it would do]."* |
| 7 | [ ] **Step 2: VALIDATE Written** -- You have written 1-3 sentences that explicitly confirm the reader's feelings are justified. Contains "not your fault" or equivalent. Does NOT contradict their beliefs. | *"You're not [negative self-judgment they fear]. And you're not wrong for feeling [their dominant emotion]. The [emotion] you feel -- that sense that [specific frustration] -- is completely justified."* |
| 8 | [ ] **Step 3: REDIRECT Written** -- You have written 1-2 sentences that introduce the NEW information (the MUP) without mentioning the product. Feels like a revelation, not a pitch. | *"But here's what [authority figures] never told you -- because most of them don't know it yet: the problem was never [what they think it is]."* |
| 9 | [ ] **Step 4: BRIDGE Written** -- You have written 1-2 sentences connecting the redirect to the solution. Feels inevitable, not commercial. | *"And once you understand what's ACTUALLY [causing/blocking/creating] the [problem] -- and it's something [surprising detail] -- the path to [desired outcome] becomes [adjective: simple/clear/obvious]."* |
| 10 | [ ] **40% Rule Verified** -- Steps 1-2 (Mirror + Validate) comprise at least 40% of the total opening word count. Measured, not estimated. | Count the words. Steps 1-2 = X words. Total opening = Y words. X/Y must be >= 0.40. If not, expand Mirror and Validate sections. |
| 11 | [ ] **No Product Mention in Bridge** -- The product name, product type, and solution category do NOT appear anywhere in Steps 1-3. Only Step 4 begins to hint at a path forward. | Search your bridge text for the product name and any synonym. If found in Steps 1-3, remove it. The bridge is about the READER'S world, not your product. |
| 12 | [ ] **VOC Language Used** -- At least 3 phrases in the bridge are pulled directly from VOC research (exact quotes or very close paraphrases). | Underline the VOC phrases in your bridge. If fewer than 3, replace your language with theirs. |
| 13 | [ ] **Specific Failed Attempts Referenced** -- The Mirror step mentions at least 2 specific things the reader has tried and failed with. | Not "you've tried everything" (vague). "You've tried [specific thing 1] and [specific thing 2]" (specific). |

## Category 3: Six Prime Motivators Audit

Every piece of copy must tap at least one of Collier's 6 Prime Motivators.
The strongest copy taps 2-3. Audit your copy against all six.

| # | Check | Diagnostic Question |
|---|-------|-------------------|
| 14 | [ ] **Primary Motivator Identified** -- You can name the ONE dominant motivator your copy taps. It is the motivator with the HIGHEST intensity for this specific audience. | Which of the 6 does this audience feel MOST intensely? (1) Love/belonging, (2) Gain/greed, (3) Duty/obligation, (4) Pride/recognition, (5) Self-indulgence/pleasure, (6) Self-preservation/fear. The answer must come from VOC, not assumption. |
| 15 | [ ] **Secondary Motivator Activated** -- Your copy taps at least ONE additional motivator beyond the primary. The secondary motivator reinforces the primary, not competes with it. | Does a second motivator appear in your copy? Example: Primary = Self-preservation (fear of deafness), Secondary = Love (not being present for family conversations). Both pull in the SAME direction. |
| 16 | [ ] **Motivator Is SPECIFIC** -- The motivator is expressed through specific, concrete scenarios -- not abstract concepts. | "Self-preservation" is abstract. "Terrified that the ringing means your brain is deteriorating" is specific. "Love" is abstract. "Wanting your husband to look at you the way he did on your wedding night" is specific. |
| 17 | [ ] **Motivator Appears in Opening** -- The primary motivator is activated within the first 20% of the copy. It does not wait for the middle or end. | Check: where does the motivator first appear? If it is buried in the second half, restructure. The reader's desire must be BURNING from the beginning. |
| 18 | [ ] **No Motivator Conflict** -- The motivators activated in your copy do not pull in opposite directions. (Example conflict: "relax and enjoy" vs "work harder than ever.") | List your motivators. Do they align? Can the reader pursue both simultaneously? If not, choose one and remove the other. |
| 19 | [ ] **Motivator Matches VOC** -- The motivator(s) you chose are confirmed by VOC research. You have quotes from real people expressing this specific desire. | Find 3+ VOC quotes that express the motivator you're tapping. If you cannot find them, the motivator may be YOUR assumption, not the reader's reality. |

## Category 4: Desire Channeling Worksheet

Desire is channeled, not created. Use this worksheet to map the desire
landscape before writing.

| # | Check | How to Complete |
|---|-------|-----------------|
| 20 | [ ] **Desire Stack Created** -- You have a ranked list of every desire the product fulfills, ordered from strongest to weakest intensity. Minimum 5 desires listed. | List every outcome the reader wants. Rank by emotional intensity (not importance to YOU). The #1 desire is what leads your copy. #2-3 reinforce. #4+ deepen the vision section. |
| 21 | [ ] **Primary Desire Is Pre-Existing** -- The #1 desire on your stack existed in the reader BEFORE your copy. It is something they already want, not something your copy convinced them to want. | Test: Would the reader nod "yes" if asked "Do you want [this]?" before reading your copy? If they need YOUR copy to create the desire, it is not pre-existing. Find one that is. |
| 22 | [ ] **Connection Made Clear** -- Your copy explicitly shows how the product is the SHORTEST, SAFEST, MOST CERTAIN path from where the reader is to the fulfillment of their #1 desire. | The reader must see: "THIS product = THAT desire fulfilled." Not vaguely. CLEARLY. With specifics about how the product delivers the desired outcome. |
| 23 | [ ] **Alternatives Feel Inferior** -- After reading your copy, the reader would feel that other solutions (competitors, DIY, doing nothing) are clearly worse paths to the same desire. | Not by attacking competitors. By making YOUR path so vivid and certain that alternatives fade by comparison. "Why would I try THAT when THIS is available?" |
| 24 | [ ] **Desire Language Used** -- The desire is expressed in the reader's language, not yours. "Sleeping through the night" not "improved sleep quality." "Making my wife smile again" not "enhanced relationship outcomes." | Check every desire statement in your copy. Is it in THEIR voice or YOUR voice? Replace your voice with theirs everywhere. |
| 25 | [ ] **Desire Reinforced 3+ Times** -- The primary desire appears at least 3 times throughout the copy -- in the opening, in the middle, and near the close. Each appearance uses different words but the same underlying desire. | Mark every instance where the primary desire is referenced. If fewer than 3, add reinforcement points. The reader should feel the desire BUILDING, not fading. |

## Category 5: Specificity Audit (10 Checkpoints)

Every major claim in your copy must pass this specificity audit. Vague claims
create suspicion. Specific claims create belief.

| # | Check | Standard |
|---|-------|----------|
| 26 | [ ] **Named Person Present** -- At least one specific person is named in the copy (not "a doctor" but "Dr. Klaus Richter" or "Maria, 47, from Goiania"). | Every story, testimonial, or case reference must have a NAME. Anonymous claims have zero credibility. |
| 27 | [ ] **Non-Round Numbers Used** -- Every number in the copy is non-round (87%, not 90%. $347, not $350. 23 days, not 3 weeks. 2,847 people, not "thousands"). | Search for round numbers: 10, 20, 50, 100, 500, 1000. Replace each with a measured, non-round equivalent. Round = invented. Non-round = measured. |
| 28 | [ ] **Geographic Anchor Present** -- At least one specific location is named (city, institution, country, region). | "Researchers in Germany" is moderate. "Researchers at the University of Heidelberg" is strong. "Dr. Richter's lab on the third floor of the Heidelberg audiology center" is vivid. |
| 29 | [ ] **Temporal Anchor Present** -- At least one specific time reference appears (date, time of day, day of week, month, year). | "Recently" is banned. "In March 2024" is moderate. "At 3:17 AM on a Tuesday in March" is strong. Time anchors signal lived experience. |
| 30 | [ ] **Sensory Detail Included** -- At least one detail engages a sense beyond sight/sound (touch, smell, taste, temperature, texture, physical sensation). | "She felt better" is abstract. "The pressure behind her eyes released, like a fist unclenching" is sensory. Sensory details bypass skepticism. |
| 31 | [ ] **Irrelevant Vivid Detail Present** -- At least one detail is included that is technically unnecessary but makes the scene feel REAL (what someone was wearing, what they were drinking, what was on TV). | "She was pouring her second cup of coffee -- the instant kind, because her husband refuses to buy a real machine -- when she realized the silence." The irrelevant detail PROVES it happened. |
| 32 | [ ] **Result Specificity Verified** -- Every result claim is specific and measurable, not vague and qualitative. | "Improved" is banned. "Blood pressure dropped from 180/120 to 124/81 in 14 days" is specific. "Felt better" is banned. "Slept 7 hours straight for the first time in 3 years" is specific. |
| 33 | [ ] **Data + Narrative Specificity Present** -- The copy contains BOTH data specificity (numbers, stats, measurements) AND narrative specificity (vivid scenes, sensory details, human moments). | Both faces required. Data alone = clinical. Narrative alone = anecdotal. Together = unshakable belief. Check that both types appear. |
| 34 | [ ] **3-of-5 Rule Met on Major Claims** -- Every major claim has at least 3 of the 5 specificity anchors: Name, Number, Time, Place, Vivid Detail. | List your 3 biggest claims. For each, count how many anchors are present. If any claim has fewer than 3, add specifics until it passes. |
| 35 | [ ] **Zero Vague Quantifiers** -- No instances of: "many people," "thousands," "a lot of," "most," "some," "several," "various," "numerous," "countless." Each replaced with a specific number or named example. | Search for every vague quantifier. Replace each one. "Many people" becomes "2,847 people across 14 countries." "Most doctors" becomes "Dr. Richter and his team of 7 audiologists." |

## Category 6: Letter Structure Template

Use this to verify the structure of your letter follows Collier's 8-section
architecture with appropriate word count allocation.

| # | Check | Structural Verification |
|---|-------|----------------------|
| 36 | [ ] **Hook Present (5-8%)** -- The opening enters the reader's existing conversation. Does NOT mention the product. Makes the reader think "this is about ME." | Target: 50-100 words. If the hook mentions your product, credentials, or company, rewrite. The hook belongs to the READER. |
| 37 | [ ] **Bridge Present (15-20%)** -- The Empathy Bridge follows the 4-step architecture: Mirror, Validate, Redirect, Connect. 40% rule satisfied. | Target: 150-250 words. Verify the 4 steps are present in order. Verify Steps 1-2 are >= 40% of total opening. |
| 38 | [ ] **Story Present (15-20%)** -- A transformation narrative carries the selling message. The reader sees themselves in the story. Contains specific details (names, places, numbers). | Target: 150-250 words. The story must feature someone the reader IDENTIFIES with -- not a celebrity, not the company founder, but someone like THEM. |
| 39 | [ ] **Evidence Present (10-15%)** -- Proof section with specifics: numbers, names, results, mechanism explanation. Answers "does it REALLY work?" | Target: 100-175 words. Check for named people, measured results, institutional references, mechanism clarity. |
| 40 | [ ] **Vision Present (10-15%)** -- Future-pacing section painting life AFTER the purchase. Specific and sensory, not abstract. | Target: 100-175 words. The reader must FEEL the future. "Imagine waking up and... silence. Just silence." Not "your life will improve." |
| 41 | [ ] **Offer Present (10-12%)** -- Clear statement of what they get and what it costs. Components listed individually. Value exceeds price perception. | Target: 100-150 words. Every component named. Perceived value >> price. |
| 42 | [ ] **Push Present (8-10%)** -- Specific, logical reasons why delay costs the reader something real. Not generic urgency. | Target: 75-125 words. "Act now" is banned. Specific consequences of waiting are required. |
| 43 | [ ] **Close Present (5-8%)** -- Explicit, step-by-step instructions on what to do next. Zero ambiguity. | Target: 50-100 words. Not "click here." But "Click the green button below. On the next page, select your package. Enter your details. Your [product] ships within 24 hours." |
| 44 | [ ] **Sections in Order** -- The 8 sections appear in sequence: Hook > Bridge > Story > Evidence > Vision > Offer > Push > Close. No section is skipped or reordered. | Review the full copy structure. Mark where each section begins and ends. If any section is missing or out of order, restructure. |

## Category 7: "Enter the Conversation" Opening Formulas (8 Types)

Verify which opening formula was used and whether it was executed correctly.

| # | Check | Formula Verification |
|---|-------|---------------------|
| 45 | [ ] **Opening Type Selected** -- You consciously chose one of the 8 entry types BEFORE writing, based on the reader's current state (not randomly). | The 8 types: (1) Failed Solutions, (2) Current Suffering, (3) Secret Fear, (4) Recurring Moment, (5) Wrong Belief, (6) Tribal Selection, (7) Overheard Truth, (8) Time-Stamped Pain. Which did you choose? WHY? |
| 46 | [ ] **Opening Matches Reader State** -- The chosen formula matches the reader's documented emotional state and awareness level. | Failed Solutions works for Solution Aware readers. Secret Fear works for Problem Aware. Wrong Belief works for Unaware/Problem Aware. Verify alignment with awareness level. |
| 47 | [ ] **Opening Is Market-Specific** -- The opening could NOT be used in a different market without significant rewriting. It contains details specific to THIS audience and THIS problem. | Test: swap the opening into a competitor's copy. Does it still work? If yes, it is not specific enough. Add details that make it IMPOSSIBLE to transplant. |
| 48 | [ ] **First Sentence Under 15 Words** -- The very first sentence is short, punchy, and impossible to skip. | Count the words. If more than 15, split or cut. The first sentence is a HOOK, not a paragraph. |
| 49 | [ ] **Micro-Commitment Created** -- The opening creates an internal "yes" from the reader within the first 2-3 sentences. The reader nods, agrees, or recognizes themselves. | Read the opening as the reader. Do you nod? Do you think "yes, that's me"? If the reaction is neutral, the opening is not entering their conversation. |
| 50 | [ ] **"I" Does Not Appear First** -- The first word of the copy is NOT "I." The opening focuses on the reader ("You," "Your," or a description of their world), not the writer. | Check the first word. If "I," rewrite. The opening belongs to the reader. You enter THEIR world; you do not invite them into yours. |

## Scoring

| Checks Passed | Rating | Action |
|---------------|--------|--------|
| 45-50 | COLLIER-GRADE | Ship with confidence. The reader's conversation has been entered. |
| 35-44 | STRONG | Address remaining gaps, particularly in Categories 1-2, then ship. |
| 25-34 | ADEQUATE | Significant rewrite needed. Likely failing on empathy bridge or specificity. |
| 15-24 | WEAK | Major structural issues. Return to VOC research and rebuild from diagnostic. |
| Below 15 | REJECT | Start over. Read collier.md F1-F6 before attempting again. |

## Quick Reference: The 8 Opening Formulas

| # | Type | Formula | Best For |
|---|------|---------|----------|
| 1 | **Failed Solutions** | "If you've tried [X, Y, Z] and nothing worked..." | Solution Aware (frustrated) |
| 2 | **Current Suffering** | "Right now, at this moment, you're dealing with..." | Problem Aware (in pain) |
| 3 | **Secret Fear** | "There's something you haven't told anyone..." | Problem Aware (afraid) |
| 4 | **Recurring Moment** | "Every [time], the same thing happens..." | Problem Aware (pattern recognition) |
| 5 | **Wrong Belief** | "You've been told [common belief]. That's not true." | Unaware / Problem Aware (paradigm shift) |
| 6 | **Tribal Selection** | "If you're the kind of person who [trait]..." | Most Aware (identity-based) |
| 7 | **Overheard Truth** | "The other day, someone said something that stopped me..." | Problem Aware (curiosity) |
| 8 | **Time-Stamped Pain** | "Last night at [time], you probably..." | Problem Aware (vivid pain) |

## Quick Reference: The 6 Prime Motivators

| # | Motivator | Core Question | Copy Application |
|---|-----------|---------------|-----------------|
| 1 | **Love** | "Will this help me connect with / be loved by [person]?" | Show restored or deepened relationships |
| 2 | **Gain** | "Will this give me more money / status / advantage?" | Show specific material improvements |
| 3 | **Duty** | "Is this something I SHOULD do for [family/self/others]?" | Make inaction feel irresponsible |
| 4 | **Pride** | "Will others admire / recognize / respect me for this?" | Show how others will see them differently |
| 5 | **Self-Indulgence** | "Will this make my life easier / more pleasurable?" | Paint a picture of effortless results |
| 6 | **Self-Preservation** | "Will this protect me from [threat/loss/deterioration]?" | Show what they LOSE by not acting |

## Usage Notes

- Run Category 1 (Reader Entry Diagnostic) BEFORE writing. It is a prerequisite, not a post-check.
- Run Category 2 (Empathy Bridge) DURING construction of the bridge section.
- Run Categories 3-5 AFTER completing the first draft.
- Run Category 6 (Letter Structure) to verify overall architecture.
- Run Category 7 AFTER selecting and writing your opening formula.
- Cross-reference with `collier.md` for full framework detail and examples.
- Cross-reference with VOC research files for exact reader language.
- This checklist is REQUIRED for all sections assigned to Collier: lead, reader_entry, empathy_bridge, letter_opening.

# Ogilvy Brand Response Checklist - T0_DIAGNOSIS

> **Expert:** David Ogilvy
> **Tier:** T0_DIAGNOSIS
> **Threshold:** 85/100 to PASS
> **Use:** Pre-flight audit for any copy that must build brand while selling.
> Applies to: headlines, VSLs, landing pages, print ads, criativos, email sequences.

---

## How to Use This Checklist

1. Complete each section honestly, scoring each criterion
2. Sum category scores
3. Check total against decision matrix
4. If REVISE or REWRITE, address specific failing categories first
5. Re-audit after revisions (never skip re-audit)

---

## Scoring Matrix

### 1. Research Foundation (15 points)

> "I am helpless without research material."
> Copy written without research is guessing. Expensive guessing.

| Criterion | 0 pts | 5 pts | 8 pts | 15 pts |
|-----------|-------|-------|-------|--------|
| Research depth | No research done | Brief product review | Product + competitor study | Full immersion (product, competitors, consumers, 20yr ad history) |

**Checklist items:**

- [ ] Product studied in depth (specs, process, ingredients, mechanism)
- [ ] Competitor advertising reviewed (at minimum past 5 years)
- [ ] Consumer research consumed (VOC, reviews, forums, complaints)
- [ ] Product used or experienced personally (when possible)
- [ ] At least ONE specific fact from research appears in headline
- [ ] Body copy contains facts discoverable only through research
- [ ] No claim in the copy lacks a factual basis from research

**Red flags (automatic 0):**
- Copy written from brief alone without additional research
- All facts could be found on the product's own marketing page
- No consumer language present (only marketer language)

**Score: ___/15**

### 2. Headline Quality (20 points)

> "When you have written your headline, you have spent eighty cents out of your dollar."
> Five times as many people read the headline as read the body copy.

| Criterion | 0 pts | 5 pts | 10 pts |
|-----------|-------|-------|--------|
| Benefit present? | No clear benefit | Implied benefit | Explicit, specific benefit |
| Specificity? | Generic/vague | Some detail | Specific fact, number, or detail |

**Checklist items:**

- [ ] Headline contains a clear BENEFIT or NEWS element
- [ ] Headline is 10+ words (long headlines sell more)
- [ ] Brand or product name appears in the headline
- [ ] Headline selects the right reader (wrong readers self-exclude)
- [ ] At least 5 alternative headlines were written before selection
- [ ] Headline contains a SPECIFIC detail (number, name, process)
- [ ] Headline could NOT be used by a competitor without modification
- [ ] Headline does not use puns, double meanings, or wordplay
- [ ] Headline is not "blind" (could be about anything)
- [ ] Headline promises something the body copy delivers

**Headline type classification (mark one):**

| Type | Present? | Effectiveness Rank |
|------|----------|--------------------|
| Promise of benefit | [ ] | 1 (highest) |
| News announcement | [ ] | 2 |
| Curiosity with substance | [ ] | 3 |
| How-to | [ ] | 4 |
| Question that implies benefit | [ ] | 5 |
| Testimonial headline | [ ] | 6 |
| Command headline | [ ] | 7 |

**Instant-fail triggers:**
- Pun or wordplay in headline
- No benefit AND no news AND no curiosity
- Headline works equally well for competitor product
- Headline shorter than 5 words with no justification

**Score: ___/20**

### 3. Big Idea Test (15 points)

> "It takes a big idea to attract attention. Unless your advertising
> contains a big idea, it will pass like a ship in the night."
> Fewer than 1 in 100 campaigns contain a big idea.

| Question | YES (3 pts) | NO (0 pts) |
|----------|-------------|------------|
| Did it make me gasp? | [ ] | [ ] |
| Do I wish I had thought of it? | [ ] | [ ] |
| Is it unique? | [ ] | [ ] |
| Does it fit the strategy perfectly? | [ ] | [ ] |
| Could it run for 30 years? | [ ] | [ ] |

**Evaluation notes:**

- [ ] The central idea can be stated in ONE sentence
- [ ] The idea is inseparable from the product (cannot be borrowed)
- [ ] The idea surprises AND makes logical sense simultaneously
- [ ] The idea is simple enough to explain to someone in 10 seconds
- [ ] The idea creates jealousy in competitors (not just admiration)

**Scoring guide:**
- 5/5 YES = 15 points (Big Idea confirmed)
- 4/5 YES = 12 points (Strong idea, refine the weak dimension)
- 3/5 YES = 9 points (Concept, not yet a big idea)
- 2/5 YES = 5 points (Needs fundamental rethinking)
- 1/5 or 0/5 = 0 points (Discard and start fresh)

**Score: ___/15**

### 4. Specificity Audit (15 points)

> "Specific facts persuade. Adjectives puff."
> The Rolls-Royce Method: replace every adjective with a fact.

| Criterion | 0 pts | 5 pts | 8 pts | 15 pts |
|-----------|-------|-------|-------|--------|
| Fact density | Mostly adjectives | Mixed | Facts dominate | Zero adjectives without accompanying facts |

**Adjective audit (count each):**

| Category | Count |
|----------|-------|
| Adjectives WITHOUT facts | ___ (target: 0) |
| Adjectives WITH supporting facts | ___ |
| Specific numbers (non-round) | ___ (target: 3+) |
| Named sources/references | ___ (target: 2+) |
| Process descriptions | ___ (target: 2+) |
| Verifiable details | ___ (target: 5+) |

**Checklist items:**

- [ ] No unsupported adjective survives (each one paired with a fact)
- [ ] At least 3 specific, non-round numbers in the copy
- [ ] At least 2 named sources (person, institution, publication)
- [ ] At least 2 process descriptions (how something is made/done)
- [ ] Claims are stated as FACTS, not as opinions
- [ ] Engineer's language present (not just marketer's language)
- [ ] Reader could verify claims independently if she wanted to

**The Adjective Replacement Test:**
Pick the 3 strongest adjectives in the copy. For each:
1. Can it be replaced by a specific number? [ ]
2. Can it be replaced by a process description? [ ]
3. Can it be replaced by a named reference? [ ]
If YES to any, replace it. If NO to all three, the adjective may stay.

**Instant-fail triggers:**
- Body copy has more adjectives than facts
- Zero specific numbers in entire piece
- "Best," "finest," "revolutionary" used without evidence
- Claims that rely on the reader trusting your word alone

**Score: ___/15**

### 5. Brand Image Consistency (10 points)

> "Every advertisement is part of the long-term investment in the
> personality of the brand."

| Criterion | 0 pts | 3 pts | 5 pts | 10 pts |
|-----------|-------|-------|-------|--------|
| Brand consistency | No defined personality | Some consistency | Mostly consistent | Perfect alignment with brand personality |

**Checklist items:**

- [ ] Brand personality is DEFINED before evaluation (documented)
- [ ] Tone matches established brand personality exactly
- [ ] Visual style (if applicable) is consistent with brand assets
- [ ] This piece could be recognized as this brand with logo removed
- [ ] No element contradicts the established brand personality
- [ ] Copy adds to the brand equity (does not merely spend it)
- [ ] Language register is consistent with brand character

**Brand Personality Sharpness Test:**

| Question | Answer |
|----------|--------|
| If this brand were a person, who would they be? | ___ |
| What is the ONE adjective that defines it? | ___ |
| What would this brand NEVER do? | ___ |
| Does this copy do anything the brand would never do? | YES/NO |

**Scoring guide:**
- Brand undefined = maximum 5 points (define brand first)
- Brand defined + copy consistent = 8-10 points
- Brand defined + copy inconsistent = 0-3 points

**Score: ___/10**

### 6. Long Copy Structure (10 points)

> "The more you tell, the more you sell."
> Long copy outsells short copy when the reader is interested.

| Criterion | 0 pts | 3 pts | 5 pts | 10 pts |
|-----------|-------|-------|-------|--------|
| Copy sufficiency | Too short for subject | Adequate | Thorough | Every relevant fact included, zero filler |

**Checklist items:**

- [ ] Copy length is appropriate to the product/price (higher price = longer copy)
- [ ] No relevant fact was omitted for brevity
- [ ] Every paragraph advances the sale (no filler paragraphs)
- [ ] Copy follows editorial style (not advertising style)
- [ ] Paragraphs are 3-4 sentences maximum
- [ ] First paragraph hooks the reader into the second
- [ ] Subheadings tell the story even if body copy is skipped
- [ ] Copy reads like journalism, not like advertising

**Long Copy Justification Test:**
- What is the product price? ___
- What is the reader's awareness level? ___
- What is the commitment level required? ___

| Price/Commitment | Minimum Copy Length |
|------------------|-------------------|
| Low-ticket (<$50) | 300-500 words |
| Mid-ticket ($50-500) | 500-2000 words |
| High-ticket ($500+) | 2000-5000+ words |
| Subscription/SaaS | 1000-3000 words |

### 7. Direct Response Discipline (10 points)

> "Direct response was my first love. Later it became my secret weapon."
> Every advertisement should be measurable.

| Criterion | 0 pts | 3 pts | 5 pts | 10 pts |
|-----------|-------|-------|-------|--------|
| Response mechanics | No CTA or measurement | Basic CTA | CTA + tracking | Full DR discipline (CTA, tracking, offer, measurement) |

**Checklist items:**

- [ ] Clear, specific call to action present
- [ ] Offer is defined (what the reader gets for responding)
- [ ] Response is trackable and measurable
- [ ] Captions used under all images (miniature ads)
- [ ] Every CTA is linked to a specific benefit or consequence
- [ ] Risk reversal present (guarantee, trial, money-back)
- [ ] Urgency or reason-to-act-now present (with reason WHY)

**Direct Response Accountability Test:**

| Element | Present? | Measurable? |
|---------|----------|-------------|
| Headline effectiveness | [ ] | [ ] |
| Body copy engagement | [ ] | [ ] |
| CTA click-through | [ ] | [ ] |
| Conversion | [ ] | [ ] |
| Revenue attribution | [ ] | [ ] |

### 8. Reader Respect (5 points)

> "The consumer is not a moron. She is your wife."

| Criterion | 0 pts | 2 pts | 5 pts |
|-----------|-------|-------|-------|
| Respect level | Condescending or hyped | Mostly respectful | Treats reader as intelligent equal |

**Checklist items:**

- [ ] No ALL CAPS in body copy
- [ ] No excessive exclamation marks (maximum 1-2 in entire piece)
- [ ] No condescending language ("It's so easy even you can...")
- [ ] No jargon without explanation
- [ ] No hype words: "revolutionary", "incredible", "unbelievable"
- [ ] Reader is addressed as an intelligent adult making a decision
- [ ] Copy informs rather than pressures
- [ ] If it reads like advertising, it has been rewritten as information

**The Wife Test:**
Would you feel comfortable showing this copy to your spouse,
explaining that this is how you talk to customers?
- YES = Pass
- NO = Rewrite until YES

**Score: ___/5**

## Category-Specific Remediation

If a specific category fails, use these targeted remediation steps:

### Research Foundation < 10/15
1. Stop writing. Return to research phase.
2. Read competitor advertisements from past 5+ years.
3. Consume consumer research (VOC, reviews, forums).
4. Use or experience the product personally.
5. Only resume writing when you can cite 10+ specific facts from research.

### Headline < 13/20
1. Write 20 additional headlines (not variations -- new approaches).
2. For each, verify: benefit present? Brand name? Specific detail?
3. Apply the Logo Test: can competitor use this headline? If yes, discard.
4. Get opinions from 3+ people outside the project.
5. Consider split-run testing for top 3 candidates.

### Big Idea < 9/15
1. Revisit research for the overlooked fact that "catches fire."
2. Apply the Gasp Test honestly: did you gasp? If not, keep looking.
3. Take a break. Load the unconscious. Return fresh.
4. Consider: is this a tactic or a true idea? Tactics cannot run 30 years.
5. The idea must be inseparable from the product.

### Specificity < 10/15
1. List every adjective in the copy.
2. For each adjective, find a FACT to replace or support it.
3. Add at least 3 non-round numbers from research.
4. Add at least 2 named sources or references.
5. Add at least 2 process descriptions (how something is made/done).
6. Re-read: does it sound like an engineer talking, or a marketer?

### Brand Image < 6/10
1. Document brand personality if not defined (STOP until this is done).
2. Compare this copy's tone against last 5 brand communications.
3. Remove any element that contradicts established personality.
4. Apply the Logo Removal Test: recognizable without the logo?
5. Ensure tone consistency down to word choice and sentence rhythm.

### Long Copy < 6/10
1. List all relevant product facts not yet in the copy.
2. Remove filler paragraphs (add fact paragraphs instead).
3. Verify each paragraph advances the sale.
4. Restructure as editorial (not advertising) layout.
5. Add subheadings that tell the story independently.

### Direct Response < 6/10
1. Define the specific offer (what the reader gets for responding).
2. Add a clear, specific CTA (not "Learn More" -- what happens next?).
3. Add tracking to every CTA.
4. Add captions under all images.
5. Add risk reversal (guarantee, trial).
6. Add reason-to-act-now with honest justification.

### Reader Respect < 3/5
1. Remove all ALL CAPS from body copy.
2. Reduce exclamation marks to maximum 2 in entire piece.
3. Remove jargon or add immediate plain-language explanations.
4. Read aloud: does it sound like explaining to an intelligent friend?
5. Apply The Wife Test.

# Chaperon Email Arc -- Master Checklist

> **Source:** Andre Chaperon Voice DNA Profile
> **Use:** Validate any email sequence against Chaperon principles before delivery.
> **Ref:** `~/.claude/copy-squad/chaperon.md`

---

## 1. SOS 5-Email Structure Checklist

### Email 1: Set the Stage

- [ ] Opens in medias res (mid-action, no preamble, no greeting)
- [ ] Protagonist is established within the first 3 sentences
- [ ] Emotional stakes are clear (what is at risk, what is at tension)
- [ ] Sensory details present (time, place, physical sensation, dialogue)
- [ ] Ends with a strong open loop (impossible to ignore)
- [ ] ZERO product mention
- [ ] ZERO context-setting or "welcome to this sequence"
- [ ] Length: 200-400 words

### Email 2: Backstory + High Drama

- [ ] Rewinds to BEFORE the dramatic moment from Email 1
- [ ] Shows the "before" state with empathy (frustration, failed attempts, mounting pressure)
- [ ] Problem introduced in NARRATIVE form (not bullet points, not clinical description)
- [ ] Protagonist becomes REAL (specific details: job, city, age, habit, dialogue)
- [ ] Builds tension toward the turning point without revealing it
- [ ] Ends with another open loop (the moment BEFORE the change)
- [ ] ZERO product mention (mechanism teased at most)
- [ ] Length: 300-500 words

### Email 3: The Epiphany

- [ ] Opens by connecting to the cliffhanger from Email 2
- [ ] Builds to the moment of realization: the turning point
- [ ] Mechanism/product introduced THROUGH the story (not alongside it)
- [ ] Discovery feels EARNED (reader has waited 2 emails for this)
- [ ] Shows the immediate first result or reaction after the epiphany
- [ ] Ends with a soft open loop pointing to benefits ("But what I didn't expect...")
- [ ] Product feels like a natural revelation, NOT a recommendation
- [ ] Length: 300-600 words

### Email 4: Hidden Benefits

- [ ] Continues the story AFTER the epiphany (not a new story)
- [ ] Reveals 2-3 unexpected benefits beyond the main promise
- [ ] Each benefit is a mini-story, not a bullet point
- [ ] Secondary benefits address DIFFERENT objections than the main promise
- [ ] Benefits bypass defense mechanisms (they feel like bonuses, not claims)
- [ ] Ends with a transition toward resolution
- [ ] Social proof woven into story (not testimonial blocks)
- [ ] Length: 300-500 words

### Email 5: Urgency + CTA

- [ ] ALL open loops from the sequence are closed
- [ ] Shows protagonist's current state (the visceral "after")
- [ ] Before/after contrast is sharp and emotionally resonant
- [ ] Product/offer presented as "the same path" the protagonist took
- [ ] Urgency is NARRATIVE (not countdown timer, not artificial scarcity)
- [ ] ONE clear CTA (not multiple links)
- [ ] CTA is a natural extension of the story, not a sudden pitch
- [ ] PS reinforces CTA or adds a personal note
- [ ] Length: 300-600 words

### Sequence-Level Validation

- [ ] All 5 emails form a single continuous narrative arc
- [ ] Protagonist is consistent across all emails (same person, same voice)
- [ ] Emotional progression: tension -> empathy -> discovery -> proof -> action
- [ ] Product does NOT appear before Email 3
- [ ] Each email is valuable standalone but INCOMPLETE without the next
- [ ] Total sequence reads like a short story, not a marketing campaign

## 2. Open Loop Audit

Every email in any sequence MUST pass this audit.

### Per-Email Check

- [ ] Email contains at least 1 open loop
- [ ] Open loop is SPECIFIC (not "stay tuned" or "more soon")
- [ ] Open loop type identified: Story / Question / Tease / Mystery / Contradiction
- [ ] Open loop creates genuine cognitive tension (would YOU want to know?)
- [ ] Previous email's loop(s) addressed (closed or advanced)

### Sequence-Level Loop Management

- [ ] No more than 4 active (unclosed) loops at any point in the sequence
- [ ] Loops are staggered (not all opened in one email, not all closed in one email)
- [ ] Subject lines hint at loop resolution from previous email
- [ ] Final email of sequence closes ALL remaining loops
- [ ] No "orphan loops" (opened but never closed)

### Loop Quality Scale

| Score | Description | Action |
|-------|-------------|--------|
| 1 | Generic ("more tomorrow") | REWRITE -- no tension created |
| 2 | Mildly curious but skippable | STRENGTHEN -- add specificity |
| 3 | Genuinely curious | ACCEPTABLE for mid-sequence |
| 4 | Strong pull, hard to ignore | GOOD for most emails |
| 5 | Impossible to not open next email | REQUIRED for Email 1 and Email 3 endings |

## 3. Narrative Arc Template for Email Sequences

Use this template to MAP the arc before writing any email.

### Pre-Writing Arc Map

```
PROTAGONIST: [Who is the character? Name, age, situation]
STARTING STATE: [Where are they emotionally/physically at the start?]
PROBLEM: [What is the core conflict? What have they tried?]
TRIGGER: [What event starts the story in motion?]
EPIPHANY: [What is the discovery/turning point?]
MECHANISM: [What is the product/method? How does it connect to the epiphany?]
TRANSFORMATION: [What changed? Primary result + 2-3 hidden benefits]
END STATE: [Where is the protagonist now? How do they FEEL?]
```

### Emotional Arc (Track Across Emails)

```
Email 1: TENSION     (dramatic opening, unresolved situation)
Email 2: EMPATHY     (backstory builds identification and investment)
Email 3: HOPE        (epiphany brings relief and possibility)
Email 4: BELIEF      (hidden benefits stack evidence and reduce doubt)
Email 5: ACTION      (resolution demands participation -- the CTA)
```

### Narrative Continuity Checklist

- [ ] Each email references the previous one WITHOUT "last time on..." recaps
- [ ] Time moves forward (or backward with purpose) -- no static emails
- [ ] Protagonist's emotional state evolves email to email
- [ ] Stakes escalate through the sequence (not flat)
- [ ] Resolution in Email 5 feels INEVITABLE given the arc

## 4. Segmentation Strategy Guide

### Behavioral Tags to Implement

| Behavior | Tag | Next Action |
|----------|-----|-------------|
| Opens every email within 1 hour | `superfan` | Fast-track to offers, request testimonials |
| Opens but never clicks | `interested-not-convinced` | Send more story/proof emails |
| Clicks but never buys | `convinced-has-objections` | Trigger objection-handling sequence |
| Clicks specific topic link | `interest:[topic]` | Branch into topic-specific SOS |
| No opens for 14+ days | `cooling-off` | Wait until 30 days, then trigger win-back |
| No opens for 30+ days | `dormant` | Trigger 3-email win-back sequence |
| No engagement after win-back | `remove` | Unsubscribe for list hygiene |
| Purchased | `customer` | Move to post-purchase nurture (different voice) |
| Purchased but did not consume | `customer-inactive` | Trigger onboarding/activation sequence |

### "Choose Your Own Adventure" Setup

```
TRIGGER EMAIL:
"I've been working on something for [topic].
But I want to make sure I share the right thing with you.

Which of these sounds most like your situation right now?

-> [Link A: Description of Pain Point A]
-> [Link B: Description of Pain Point B]
-> [Link C: Description of Pain Point C]"

BRANCHING:
Click A -> SOS focused on Pain A (5 emails)
Click B -> SOS focused on Pain B (5 emails)
Click C -> SOS focused on Pain C (5 emails)
No click -> General nurture continues
```

### Micro-Commitment Chain

```
Step 1: CLICK a link in an email (lowest friction)
Step 2: READ a specific page or article
Step 3: REPLY to an email with their answer
Step 4: VISIT a sales/landing page
Step 5: ADD to cart or register for a webinar
Step 6: BUY (highest friction)

RULE: Never ask for Step 6 before accumulating Steps 1-3 minimum.
Each YES makes the next YES easier.
```

## 5. Email Voice Calibration

### DO

- [ ] Write to ONE person (your smartest friend)
- [ ] Use first person throughout ("I," never "we" unless earned)
- [ ] Short paragraphs: 1-3 sentences maximum
- [ ] Sentence fragments for impact and rhythm
- [ ] Contractions always ("I'm," "don't," "you'll")
- [ ] Use "..." for pauses and anticipation
- [ ] PS in every email (secondary loop, soft CTA, or personal aside)
- [ ] Plain text formatting (no HTML templates, no logos, no images)
- [ ] Lowercase, personal-sounding subject lines
- [ ] One core idea per email
- [ ] One CTA per email (maximum)
- [ ] End emails mid-thought when possible
- [ ] Use the reader's vocabulary (from VOC), not industry jargon

### DON'T

- [ ] "Dear [Name]" or any formal greeting -- just START
- [ ] "I want to tell you about..." -- SHOW, don't announce
- [ ] Corporate words: leverage, optimize, utilize, implement, solution
- [ ] Multiple CTAs or offers in a single email
- [ ] HTML templates, designed headers, brand logos
- [ ] Emojis in subject lines (unless native to the brand voice)
- [ ] "ICYMI" or "In case you missed it" subject lines
- [ ] Bullet-point benefit lists inside story emails
- [ ] Paragraphs longer than 4 sentences
- [ ] Starting 3+ consecutive sentences with the same word
- [ ] "We" before relationship is established
- [ ] Daily frequency during nurture phase (2-3x/week maximum)
- [ ] Newsletter-style formatting (multiple topics, sections, headers)

### Voice Self-Test

Read the email aloud. Ask these three questions:
1. Does it sound like a person talking, or a brand broadcasting?
2. Would I forward this to a friend because the STORY is good?
3. If I removed the CTA, would the email still be worth reading?

If any answer is NO, rewrite before sending.

## 6. Subject Line Formulas (10 Types)

| # | Type | Formula | Example |
|---|------|---------|---------|
| 1 | **Personal message** | lowercase, sounds like a text from a friend | "weird thing happened yesterday" |
| 2 | **Reply thread** | "re:" prefix, implies ongoing conversation | "re: that thing I mentioned" |
| 3 | **Reluctant share** | implies vulnerability or hesitation | "I almost didn't send this" |
| 4 | **Crazy confession** | breaks pattern expectations | "you're going to think I'm crazy" |
| 5 | **Direct question** | personal, specific, invites mental response | "[Name], quick question" |
| 6 | **Loop resolution** | hints at closing a previous open loop | "so here's what happened..." |
| 7 | **Contradiction** | challenges something stated in a previous email | "I was wrong about [thing]" |
| 8 | **Specificity hook** | unusual detail that demands context | "the 2 AM phone call" |
| 9 | **Incomplete sentence** | sentence fragment that MUST be completed by opening | "the one thing nobody..." |
| 10 | **Time anchor** | references a specific, recent moment | "last Tuesday, 3:47 PM" |

### Subject Line Rules

- [ ] Lowercase (unless a proper noun or intentional emphasis)
- [ ] Under 50 characters (most inboxes truncate at 50-60)
- [ ] Sounds like it came from a person, not a brand
- [ ] Creates curiosity WITHOUT being clickbait (must deliver on the promise)
- [ ] Does NOT contain: ALL CAPS words, excessive punctuation, emojis, or "FREE"
- [ ] Does NOT summarize the email (the subject line is a HOOK, not a headline)
- [ ] Tested against the "would I open this from a friend?" standard

## 7. Pre-Launch -> Launch -> Post-Launch Timeline

### Week -2 to -1: Pre-Launch (Origin Story Phase)

| Day | Email | Purpose |
|-----|-------|---------|
| -14 | Origin Story Part 1 | How the idea was born. Personal moment. Open loop. |
| -11 | Origin Story Part 2 | The problem that wouldn't go away. Deeper context. |
| -8 | Origin Story Part 3 | The breakthrough. What changed. Tease the solution. |
| -5 | Behind the Scenes | What you've been building. Process, not product. |
| -2 | "Something big tomorrow" | Maximum anticipation. All loops converging. |

### Week 0: Launch (SOS Phase)

| Day | Email | Purpose |
|-----|-------|---------|
| 0 | SOS Email 1: Set the Stage | Dramatic opening. Open loop. Link to sales page. |
| 1 | SOS Email 2: Backstory | Context and empathy. Deepen investment. |
| 2 | SOS Email 3: Epiphany | Turning point. Mechanism revealed. Link to sales page. |
| 3 | SOS Email 4: Hidden Benefits | Unexpected value. Stack proof through story. |
| 4 | SOS Email 5: Urgency + CTA | Close all loops. Primary CTA. |
| 5 | Urgency Email 1: "Last chance" | Story of someone who almost didn't act. |
| 6 | Urgency Email 2: "Doors closing" | What happens to people who DON'T act. |
| 7 | Urgency Email 3: "Final call" | Close the narrative arc with finality. |

### Week 1+: Post-Launch (Nurture Phase)

| Frequency | Type | Pattern |
|-----------|------|---------|
| Mon | Story email | Full narrative, no pitch. Relationship building. |
| Wed | Insight email | I-S-H pattern (Insight, Story, Hook). Soft CTA. |
| Fri | Value email | Teach something useful. Build authority. |
| (Occasional) | Pitch email | 1 per 3-4 non-pitch emails. Always story-wrapped. |

### Week 5+: Win-Back (If Needed)

| Trigger | Email | Purpose |
|---------|-------|---------|
| 30 days no opens | Win-back 1 | "I noticed something..." Personal. No pitch. |
| +3 days | Win-back 2 | Fresh story with genuine value. Prove worth reading. |
| +3 days | Win-back 3 | "Should I keep emailing you?" Direct. Easy unsubscribe. |
| No response | Remove | Clean list. Protect deliverability. |

## Quick Reference: Sequence Ratios

| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Value-to-pitch ratio | 3:1 (nurture) |
| Open loops per email | 2-3 opened, 1-2 closed |
| Max active loops | 4 simultaneously |
| Nurture frequency | 2-3 emails per week |
| Launch SOS length | 5-7 emails |
| Pre-launch duration | 7-14 days |
| Win-back trigger | 30 days no engagement |
| Email word count | 200-600 (varies by type) |
| Subject line length | Under 50 characters |
| CTAs per email | 1 (maximum) |

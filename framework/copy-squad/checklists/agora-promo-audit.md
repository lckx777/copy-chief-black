> **Source:** Power of One, Great Leads (6 Lead Types), Agora Control System, $1B Promo Structure, Fascination System, Proof Stacking Method, End of America archetype, Agora Copy Training methodology
> **Use:** Validate any financial promotion, magalog, advertorial, or VSL against Agora's billion-dollar standards. Score must reach threshold before production.

## 0. Quick Diagnostic (5-Second Check)

Before running the full audit, answer these five questions:

1. **Can you state the Big Idea in one sentence?** [ ] YES / [ ] NO
2. **Is the lead type matched to prospect awareness level?** [ ] YES / [ ] NO
3. **Are there 20+ fascinations with specific numbers?** [ ] YES / [ ] NO
4. **Is there a named mechanism with proof cascade?** [ ] YES / [ ] NO
5. **Would this promo beat the current control?** [ ] YES / [ ] NO

If ANY answer is NO, fix that FIRST before running the full audit.

## 1. Big Idea Validation

### 1A: Power of One (5 Elements)

- [ ] ONE Good Idea: Statable in a single sentence without clarification
- [ ] ONE Core Emotion: Dominant emotional driver is clear (fear OR greed, not both simultaneously)
- [ ] ONE Desirable Benefit: Single outcome that touches the prospect's core psyche
- [ ] ONE Captivating Story: Narrative that carries the idea from abstract to visceral
- [ ] ONE Inevitable Action: CTA feels like the only rational response to the idea

**Big Idea Statement:** ___

**Score: ___/5**

### 1B: Big Idea Quality Test

- [ ] IMMEDIATELY understandable: No explanation needed, reader grasps it in seconds
- [ ] EMOTIONALLY compelling: Creates instant visceral reaction (fear, excitement, outrage)
- [ ] TIMELY: Connected to current events the reader already knows about
- [ ] CONTRARIAN: Challenges conventional wisdom or mainstream financial narrative
- [ ] CREATES A VILLAIN: Explains who or what is responsible (institution, policy, hidden force)
- [ ] LEADS TO THE PRODUCT: The product is the inevitable answer to the big idea
- [ ] SUSTAINABLE: The idea will remain important and exciting for the life of the promo
- [ ] PROPRIETARY: Competitor cannot use this exact idea without adaptation

**Score: ___/8**

### 1C: Big Idea Stress Test

- [ ] Remove the big idea from the promo. Does the copy collapse? If it still works, the idea is not central enough.
- [ ] Could a competing newsletter use this EXACT big idea? If yes, it is not proprietary enough.
- [ ] Is the big idea the FIRST thing a subscriber would tell a friend? If not, it is not memorable enough.
- [ ] Does the big idea make the reader feel SMARTER? If not, it is not compelling enough.

### 4B: Proof Stacking Rules

- [ ] Every major claim has proof ADJACENT (no distance between claim and evidence)
- [ ] Minimum 3 proof types stacked per major claim
- [ ] Proof intensity ESCALATES throughout the promo
- [ ] Strongest proof is deployed for the biggest claim
- [ ] Copy leads with moderate claims backed by strong proof (credibility ramp)
- [ ] Proof feels effortless -- like there is MORE evidence than fits in the promo
- [ ] Every proof element includes specific details: names, numbers, dates, institutions
- [ ] No proof uses "studies show" without naming the specific study
- [ ] No proof uses "experts say" without naming the specific expert

## 5. Offer Architecture Checks

### 5A: Offer Stack Completeness

- [ ] Free Report (The Bribe) is present and positioned as the PRIMARY motivator
- [ ] Free Report has a compelling, specific title
- [ ] Newsletter subscription is positioned as the VEHICLE, not the product
- [ ] Minimum 2 bonus reports are stacked
- [ ] Each bonus addresses a different concern or desire
- [ ] Model portfolio or actionable tool is included (if applicable)
- [ ] Alert service or ongoing engagement mechanism is included (if applicable)
- [ ] Money-back guarantee is prominent and generous

**Score: ___/8**

### 5B: Pricing Psychology

- [ ] Price is presented in comparison to potential gain (not in isolation)
- [ ] "Pennies a day" or equivalent framing is used
- [ ] Price is compared to the cost of NOT having the information
- [ ] Tiered pricing anchors high to make the base option feel cheap (if applicable)
- [ ] The free report is the headline of the offer -- not the subscription price
- [ ] Value stack totals are presented ("$594 value, yours for just $49")

**Score: ___/6**

### 5C: Guarantee Architecture

- [ ] Guarantee is BOLD: long enough to feel generous (30-60 days minimum)
- [ ] Guarantee functions as a CONVICTION DEVICE, not just risk removal
- [ ] Guarantee language implies confidence: "If you don't agree this is the best..."
- [ ] Guarantee is placed prominently (not buried in fine print)
- [ ] Guarantee removes the reader's #1 objection

**Score: ___/5**

### 5D: Urgency and Scarcity

- [ ] Urgency is DATE-SPECIFIC, tied to a real event (not "limited time")
- [ ] The real event creating urgency is NAMED (Fed meeting, earnings date, policy change)
- [ ] Scarcity is JUSTIFIED (explained WHY availability is limited)
- [ ] Urgency is repeated at minimum 3 points in the promo
- [ ] Urgency is restated in the P.S.
- [ ] No false urgency or manufactured scarcity that could trigger compliance issues

**Score: ___/6**

## 6. Financial Compliance Guardrails

### 6A: Required Disclaimers

- [ ] Past performance disclaimer present: "Past performance does not guarantee future results"
- [ ] Past performance disclaimer is ADJACENT to every specific return claim
- [ ] Investment risk disclosure present: "All investments carry risk of loss"
- [ ] Income disclaimer present adjacent to testimonials: "Results not typical. Individual results may vary."
- [ ] Material connection disclosure present (author/publisher position disclosures)
- [ ] Hypothetical results clearly marked as hypothetical (if any backtested data is used)
- [ ] Subscription terms clearly stated: pricing, renewal terms, cancellation policy
- [ ] Disclaimers are in the body copy (not only in footer fine print)

**Score: ___/8**

### 6B: FTC Red Line Scan

- [ ] No implied GUARANTEED returns (even subtly)
- [ ] No hypothetical gains presented as actual trading results without disclosure
- [ ] No fabricated testimonials or actors presented as real customers
- [ ] No targeting of seniors or vulnerable populations with deceptive claims
- [ ] No positioning of the newsletter as "personalized financial advice"
- [ ] No suppression of material negative results from the track record
- [ ] No health claims without competent and reliable scientific evidence (if cross-selling health products)
- [ ] No dark patterns in subscription or cancellation flows

**Red Line Alert:** Any item checked YES = STOP production and consult compliance before proceeding.

**Score: ___/8 (8 = zero violations)**

- [ ] Every institutional reference (Forbes, Harvard, etc.) is accurate
- [ ] Every statistic can be sourced to a named, verifiable study or dataset
- [ ] No cherry-picking: representative time periods are used for performance data

## 7. Structure and Flow

### 7A: $1B Promo Structure Compliance

- [ ] **Section 1: Big Prediction** (5-10% of copy) -- Bold, specific claim tied to current events
- [ ] **Section 2: Fear/Greed Setup** (15-20%) -- Expand stakes, introduce villain, oscillate fear/greed
- [ ] **Section 3: Mechanism Reveal** (20-25%) -- Named mechanism, how it works, who discovered it
- [ ] **Section 4: Proof Cascade** (20-25%) -- 8+ proof elements, escalating from moderate to extraordinary
- [ ] **Section 5: Offer Architecture** (15-20%) -- Free report, bonuses, guarantee, price justification
- [ ] **Section 6: Close** (5-10%) -- Recap big idea, final oscillation, CTA, P.S.

**Score: ___/6**

### 7B: Fear-Greed Oscillation

- [ ] Opens with FEAR (more urgent than greed)
- [ ] Oscillation alternates every 500-800 words
- [ ] Each oscillation is MORE SPECIFIC than the last
- [ ] Ends on GREED (reader needs a positive path forward to act)
- [ ] Fear and greed BOTH escalate with each cycle
- [ ] Minimum 3 complete fear-greed cycles in the promo
- [ ] No section stays in one emotional register for more than 800 words

**Score: ___/7**

### 7C: Villain Architecture

- [ ] An institutional villain is clearly identified (Fed, Wall Street, Congress, Big Banks, mainstream media)
- [ ] The villain's MOTIVE is explained (why they act against the reader's interest)
- [ ] The villain's actions are supported by PROOF (not just accusations)
- [ ] The villain creates the NECESSITY for the product (without the villain, why does the reader need this?)
- [ ] The villain is powerful enough to feel threatening but not so powerful that the solution feels futile

**Score: ___/5**

### 7D: Mechanism Quality

- [ ] Mechanism has a proprietary, memorable NAME
- [ ] Mechanism explains WHY the product works (not just THAT it works)
- [ ] Mechanism is explained simply enough for a non-expert to understand
- [ ] Mechanism is backed by proof (not just asserted)
- [ ] Mechanism differentiates from every competitor (passes Logo Test)
- [ ] Mechanism creates an "aha moment" -- reader feels they understand something new
- [ ] Mechanism appears in headline, fascinations, and close (thread throughout)

**Score: ___/7**

## 8. Voice and Tone Audit

### 8A: Editorial Voice Check (Magalog/Advertorial)

- [ ] Copy reads like a financial analyst's report, not a sales pitch
- [ ] No exclamation marks in body copy (editorial forbids them)
- [ ] Data and charts are used as primary proof (not emotional appeals alone)
- [ ] Product introduction feels like a DISCOVERY within the analysis, not a pitch
- [ ] Transitions between sections feel editorial, not marketing
- [ ] If the masthead were removed, reader would think this is a real article

**Score: ___/6**

### 8B: Insider Language Check

- [ ] Reader is QUALIFIED early (made to feel selected, not targeted)
- [ ] Specific numbers used for the insider group (not "a few people" -- "47 investors")
- [ ] Real institutions, dates, and events ground the exclusivity claims
- [ ] "Us vs. them" dynamic is established (insiders vs. mainstream)
- [ ] Information feels SCARCE and TIME-SENSITIVE
- [ ] Reader feels like they are joining an elite group by the end of the lead

**Score: ___/6**

### 8C: Anti-Hype Scan

- [ ] No superlatives without proof ("the best" must be backed by data)
- [ ] No hype words: "incredible," "amazing," "revolutionary," "unbelievable"
- [ ] No generic promises: "make money fast," "financial freedom," "easy riches"
- [ ] No marketing cliches: "But wait, there's more!" / "Order now!" / "Limited time!"
- [ ] No salesperson language: "I guarantee" / "Trust me" / "I promise"
- [ ] Every enthusiasm in the copy is EARNED by preceding proof
- [ ] The copy could pass as a Wall Street Journal article in tone

**Score: ___/7**

## Appendix A: Quick Reference -- The 7 Fascination Formulas

|---|---------|----------|
| 1 | The Named Secret | "The [Proprietary Name] -- the [strategy/technique] that [specific result with number]" |

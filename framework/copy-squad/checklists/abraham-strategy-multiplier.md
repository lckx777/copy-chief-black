## Section 1: Strategy of Preeminence Audit (12-Point)

Every piece of copy and every offer must embody Preeminence.
Minimum: 9/12 to pass. Below 9 = REWRITE with advisory positioning.

| # | Check | Pass? | Why It Matters |
|---|-------|-------|----------------|
| 1 | [ ] **Client Language** -- The word "client" is used instead of "customer" throughout. Zero instances of "customer" in copy addressed to the buyer. | | "Customer" signals transaction. "Client" signals relationship. Abraham insists this distinction shapes the entire communication posture. |
| 2 | [ ] **Trusted Advisor Tone** -- The copy reads like a recommendation from someone who genuinely cares about the reader's outcome, not like a pitch from someone trying to make a sale. | | People buy from advisors they trust. If the copy sounds like it's TRYING to sell, it's not Preeminent. The reader should feel protected, not pressured. |
| 3 | [ ] **Exclusion Statement Present** -- The copy explicitly states who this is NOT for. At least one "This is not right for you if..." statement exists. | | Preeminence requires the courage to turn away the wrong buyers. Exclusion paradoxically increases trust and desire in the RIGHT buyers. |
| 4 | [ ] **Empathy Before Prescription** -- The copy demonstrates deep understanding of the reader's situation BEFORE presenting any solution. Minimum 2 paragraphs of empathy. | | A doctor who prescribes before examining is a quack. An advisor who recommends before understanding is a salesperson. Empathy MUST come first. |
| 5 | [ ] **Value Before Transaction** -- The copy delivers genuine value (insight, education, framework) BEFORE asking for anything in return. | | Preeminence means you start adding value before money changes hands. Free education demonstrates expertise and builds trust. |
| 6 | [ ] **Against-Interest Statement** -- At least one moment where the copy recommends against the product for specific situations ("I wouldn't recommend this if..."). | | This is the most powerful trust builder. Saying "don't buy this if X" communicates supreme confidence and genuine care. |
| 7 | [ ] **Outcome Focus Over Product Focus** -- The copy talks about the CLIENT'S desired outcome 3x more than it talks about the product's features. Count the ratio. | | Preeminent businesses are obsessed with client outcomes, not product features. The product is a VEHICLE, not the destination. |
| 8 | [ ] **No Desperation Signals** -- Zero instances of: "limited time only" without logistic reason, fake scarcity, countdown timers without justification, or pressure language. | | Preeminence and desperation are incompatible. Every urgency element must have a genuine, explained REASON. |
| 9 | [ ] **Leadership Position** -- The copy takes a definitive stance. No hedging, no "we believe," no "in our opinion." Clear conviction communicated authoritatively. | | Preeminent advisors LEAD. They tell clients what to do, how to do it, and why. Hedging signals uncertainty and erodes trust. |
| 10 | [ ] **Long-Term Framing** -- The copy frames the relationship as ongoing, not one-time. References to future value, continued support, or long-term results. | | Preeminence is about lifetime relationships, not one-time transactions. The copy should signal "I'm here for the long haul." |
| 11 | [ ] **Genuine Care Evident** -- A reader who showed this copy to a skeptical friend would hear "This person actually cares about helping you" not "They just want your money." | | The ultimate Preeminence test. If skeptics sense genuine care, the positioning is right. If they sense a sales pitch, it fails. |
| 12 | [ ] **Client's Best Interest Demonstrable** -- You can point to at least 3 specific places where the copy prioritizes client benefit over seller profit. | | Preeminence is not a vague feeling -- it must be DEMONSTRABLE through specific copy choices that clearly favor the client. |

### Preeminence Scoring

| Score | Rating | Action |
|-------|--------|--------|
| 12/12 | PREEMINENT | Ship with confidence -- this embodies the philosophy |
| 10-11 | STRONG | Address gaps, then ship |
| 9 | PASSING | Fix the missing elements before delivery |
| 7-8 | ADVISORY-ADJACENT | Significant rewrite needed -- the tone is off |
| Below 7 | TRANSACTIONAL | Full rewrite required. Read abraham.md Preeminence section. |

## Section 3: Risk Reversal Hierarchy Check (8-Point)

Every offer MUST include risk reversal. Minimum: Level 2. Target: Level 3+.

| # | Check | Pass? | Why It Matters |
|---|-------|-------|----------------|
| 1 | [ ] **Guarantee EXISTS** -- There is an explicit guarantee in the offer. Not implied. Not assumed. STATED. | | No guarantee = all risk on buyer = friction = lost sales. Abraham proved guarantees increase sales up to 300% with attrition rarely exceeding 2%. |
| 2 | [ ] **Guarantee is NAMED** -- The guarantee has a proprietary name (e.g., "The Triple Protection Promise"), not generic "money-back guarantee." | | Named guarantees feel more substantial, are more memorable, and become a selling point in themselves. Generic guarantees are invisible. |
| 3 | [ ] **Tied to SPECIFIC Outcome** -- The guarantee references a measurable result, not vague "satisfaction" (e.g., "if you don't see [X result] within [Y timeframe]"). | | Specific outcomes make the guarantee REAL. "Satisfaction guaranteed" is meaningless because satisfaction is undefined. |
| 4 | [ ] **Has a TIMEFRAME** -- The guarantee specifies exact duration (30 days, 90 days, 1 year, lifetime). | | Open-ended guarantees create uncertainty. A specific timeframe creates a defined "try it" window that encourages action. |
| 5 | [ ] **Placed NEAR CTA** -- The guarantee appears within 3 paragraphs of the call to action. Not buried earlier in the copy. | | Risk reversal reduces friction at the MOMENT OF DECISION. If it's too far from the CTA, the reader has already moved past the fear. |
| 6 | [ ] **Restated 3+ Times** -- The guarantee is mentioned at least 3 times across the full copy (intro area, close area, P.S.). | | Repetition ensures no reader misses it. The guarantee is too important to mention once and hope they noticed. |
| 7 | [ ] **Has REASON-WHY** -- The copy explains WHY you can offer such a strong guarantee. "I can offer this because..." | | An unexplained strong guarantee triggers suspicion ("what's the catch?"). A reason-why makes it believable and builds confidence. |
| 8 | [ ] **Level 2+ Strength** -- The guarantee is at least Level 2 on Abraham's hierarchy (specific outcome guarantee, not generic money-back). | | Level 1 guarantees are table stakes -- invisible in the market. Level 2+ actually MOVES buyers. |

### Risk Reversal Level Assessment

| Level | Name | Current Guarantee? | Upgrade Path |
|-------|------|--------------------|--------------|
| [ ] 1 | Standard ("money back") | | Tie to specific outcome + add timeframe |
| [ ] 2 | Specific (outcome + timeframe) | | Add "keep everything" + bonus |
| [ ] 3 | Better-Than-Risk-Free (refund + keep + bonus) | | Add performance gate |
| [ ] 4 | Performance-Based (pay on results) | | Add reversal of reversal |
| [ ] 5 | Total Confidence (you pay THEM if it fails) | | Maximum level -- maintain |

### Risk Reversal Scoring

| Score | Rating | Action |
|-------|--------|--------|
| 8/8 at Level 3+ | ABRAHAM-GRADE | Ship -- this removes all buyer friction |
| 7/8 at Level 2+ | STRONG | Fix the gap, then ship |
| 6/8 at Level 2 | ADEQUATE | Address missing elements |
| 5/8 or Level 1 | WEAK | Major revision -- risk is still on the buyer |
| Below 5 | ABSENT | Build guarantee from scratch (see abraham.md Framework 3) |

## Section 4: Joint Venture Evaluation Criteria (10-Point)

For any JV/endorsement/partnership copy. Minimum: 7/10 to proceed.

| # | Check | Pass? | Why It Matters |
|---|-------|-------|----------------|
| 1 | [ ] **Host Identified** -- The specific host/partner is named. Their audience size, trust level, and relevance are documented. | | JVs without a specific host are theoretical. Abraham's method requires targeting SPECIFIC partners, not "finding affiliates." |
| 2 | [ ] **Non-Competitive Verified** -- The host sells something complementary, not competitive. Their product is purchased BEFORE, DURING, or AFTER yours. | | Competitive JVs create conflict. Complementary JVs create synergy. The buyer sequence determines the ideal partner. |
| 3 | [ ] **Endorsement Written in HOST's Voice** -- The endorsement copy sounds like the HOST talking, not the beneficiary. Uses their vocabulary, references their audience's context. | | The entire power of a JV is trust transfer. If the endorsement doesn't sound like the host, the trust doesn't transfer. |
| 4 | [ ] **Exclusive Offer Created** -- There is something available ONLY through this host that isn't available anywhere else. | | Exclusivity gives the host a reason to promote and gives the audience a reason to act through this channel specifically. |
| 5 | [ ] **Performance-Based Structure** -- The host pays nothing upfront. Compensation is tied to results (per order, per inquiry, per lifetime value). | | Zero-risk for the host = higher willingness to promote. Performance structures are Abraham's cardinal rule for JVs. |
| 6 | [ ] **Authority Transfer Sequence Present** -- The copy follows: (1) Host establishes credibility, (2) Host introduces beneficiary, (3) Beneficiary makes offer. Never: beneficiary introduces themselves. | | The host's introduction IS the credibility. Skipping it means the beneficiary starts from zero trust, defeating the purpose of the JV. |
| 7 | [ ] **Host-Specific Bonus Exists** -- At least one bonus is exclusive to this host's audience. It references the host's context or adds value specific to their community. | | Generic bonuses feel generic. Host-specific bonuses prove this is a curated recommendation, not a mass blast. |
| 8 | [ ] **Referral Tracking Active** -- There is a mechanism to track which sales came from which host. Each host has a unique link, code, or identifier. | | Without tracking, you can't pay hosts accurately, can't evaluate JV performance, and can't optimize the best partnerships. |
| 9 | [ ] **Host's Reputation Protected** -- The offer includes a strong guarantee that protects both the buyer AND the host's reputation. The host won't look bad if the product disappoints. | | A JV partner who gets complaints from their audience will never promote again. Protecting the host's reputation is protecting the relationship. |
| 10 | [ ] **Reciprocal Value Offered** -- You've considered what you can offer the host BEYOND commission: audience access, product, consulting, co-marketing. | | The best JVs are multi-dimensional relationships, not one-way commission deals. Reciprocity deepens the partnership. |

### JV Quality Scoring

| Score | Rating | Action |
|-------|--------|--------|
| 9-10 | ABRAHAM-GRADE JV | Execute with confidence |
| 7-8 | STRONG | Address gaps, then execute |
| 5-6 | INCOMPLETE | Significant rework before approaching host |
| Below 5 | NOT READY | Build JV structure from scratch (see abraham.md Framework 5) |

## Section 5: Optimization-Before-Acquisition Audit (10-Point)

Before recommending or building ANY new acquisition channel.
All 10 checks are BLOCKING -- acquisition spend is premature if any fail.

| # | Check | Pass? | Why It Matters |
|---|-------|-------|----------------|
| 1 | [ ] **Attrition Rate Known** -- Customer churn/attrition rate is measured and documented. Not estimated. MEASURED. | | You cannot fix what you don't measure. Most businesses have no idea how many clients they lose per month. This number determines whether growth is real or illusory. |
| 2 | [ ] **Attrition Reduction Attempted** -- At least one attrition reduction initiative has been tested (retention sequence, check-in call, loyalty program, win-back campaign). | | Reducing attrition 10% is equivalent to acquiring 10% more customers -- at a fraction of the cost. Always plug the leak before filling the bucket. |
| 3 | [ ] **Conversion Rate Optimized** -- Current prospect-to-buyer conversion rate is known, and at least 2 split tests have been run to improve it. | | Doubling conversion rate doubles the value of ALL existing traffic. This is the highest-leverage optimization available. |
| 4 | [ ] **Upsell/Cross-Sell Exists** -- There is at least one upsell or cross-sell offer at or near the point of purchase. | | Transaction value (V multiplier) is the easiest to increase. An upsell costs almost nothing to create and can increase average order value 15-40%. |
| 5 | [ ] **Backend Sequence Active** -- Post-purchase follow-up exists: email sequence, phone call, retargeting, or subscription offer. | | Frequency (F multiplier) requires a backend. Without it, each customer relationship is a one-time event. The most profitable businesses have 5-12 backend touchpoints. |
| 6 | [ ] **Referral System Active** -- There is a systematic (not passive) referral program that incentivizes and facilitates client referrals. | | Referral clients convert higher, spend more, and churn less than any other acquisition source. This is the cheapest customer acquisition channel. |
| 7 | [ ] **Dormant List Assessed** -- Past clients who haven't purchased in 12+ months have been identified and a reactivation campaign has been considered or executed. | | Dormant clients are the most overlooked asset in business. They already know you, bought from you, and many will buy again with a simple reactivation offer. |
| 8 | [ ] **Pricing Tested** -- Price point has been tested within the last 6 months (higher price point tested, not just lower). | | Most businesses are UNDERPRICED. A 10% price increase with no volume loss is a direct 10% V multiplier improvement. Never assume current price is optimal. |
| 9 | [ ] **Performance Variation Analyzed** -- The gap between best and worst performing salespeople/channels/campaigns is known. | | Abraham's "performance variation quotient": if your best salesperson converts 3x your worst, raising the worst to average is massive hidden revenue. |
| 10 | [ ] **LTV Calculated** -- Lifetime value per client is calculated, not estimated. Includes all backend revenue, referral value, and retention timeline. | | LTV determines what you can afford to spend on acquisition. Without knowing LTV, you cannot rationally allocate acquisition budget. |

## Section 6: Power Parthenon Revenue Audit (8-Point)

Assess business resilience through revenue diversification.
Minimum: 5/8 for stable businesses. Below 5 = structural vulnerability.

| # | Check | Pass? | Why It Matters |
|---|-------|-------|----------------|
| 1 | [ ] **Revenue Sources Counted** -- Total distinct revenue sources (pillars) documented. Minimum target: 4. Ideal: 7+. Current count: _____ | | The Parthenon stands on multiple pillars. A business on 1-2 pillars is a diving board -- one shock and it collapses. |
| 2 | [ ] **No Single Pillar >40%** -- No single revenue source accounts for more than 40% of total revenue. Highest pillar: ____% | | Concentration risk. If your largest pillar is 70% of revenue and it falters, the business is in crisis. Diversification = resilience. |
| 3 | [ ] **Backend/Continuity Pillar Exists** -- There is at least one recurring revenue source (subscription, membership, retainer, continuity program). | | Recurring revenue is the most valuable pillar. It creates predictable income, increases LTV, and raises business valuation multiples. |
| 4 | [ ] **Alliance/JV Pillar Exists** -- At least one revenue source comes from partnerships, JVs, or endorsed relationships (not self-generated traffic). | | JV pillars cost zero to build and tap into established trust. They are the fastest pillar to add to any Parthenon. |
| 5 | [ ] **Referral Pillar Exists** -- A systematic (not passive) referral system generates measurable revenue. | | The cheapest customer acquisition channel, producing pre-sold buyers. Not "word of mouth" but a SYSTEM. |
| 6 | [ ] **Stress Test Passed** -- "If my largest revenue pillar disappeared overnight, would the business survive 90 days?" Answer is YES. | | If the answer is no, the business has a single point of failure. This is the most important Parthenon diagnostic question. |
| 7 | [ ] **Pillar Growth Plan** -- There is a documented plan to add at least 1 new revenue pillar in the next 90 days. | | Parthenon building is ongoing. Even healthy businesses should be adding pillars. Stagnation in diversification = future vulnerability. |
| 8 | [ ] **Pillars Are Complementary** -- Revenue sources serve different segments, channels, or needs. They don't cannibalize each other. | | 3 products sold through the same channel to the same customer = 1 pillar with 3 SKUs, not 3 pillars. True diversification requires different VALUE DELIVERY mechanisms. |

## Section 10: Referral System Evaluation (6-Point)

Specific audit for referral mechanisms. Minimum: 4/6 for any offer with
an active client base.

| # | Check | Pass? | Why It Matters |
|---|-------|-------|----------------|
| 1 | [ ] **Referral Mechanism Exists** -- There is a defined process for generating referrals. Not "we hope clients refer" but a SYSTEM with steps. | | Passive referrals are accidental. Systematic referrals are predictable. Abraham documented 93 distinct systems -- even ONE is better than hope. |
| 2 | [ ] **Timing is Right** -- Referral requests happen at the PEAK of satisfaction (right after results, right after positive experience), not at random times. | | Asking for referrals before delivering value is transactional. | 6 | [ ] **Tracking Active** -- Referral sources are tracked. You know WHICH clients refer, HOW MANY they refer, and the LIFETIME VALUE of referred clients. | | Without tracking, you cannot optimize. Top referrers should be identified and cultivated.

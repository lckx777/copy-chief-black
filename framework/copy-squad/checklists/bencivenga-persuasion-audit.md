## 1. Persuasion Equation (All 4 Components)

### 1A: Urgent Problem

- [ ] Problem is stated or dramatized within the first 10% of copy
- [ ] Urgency is FELT in the body (visceral), not just understood intellectually
- [ ] Problem is shown WORSENING over time (consequences of inaction escalate)
- [ ] Personal cost of inaction is specific (not abstract "bad things happen")
- [ ] Problem is specific to the TARGET reader (not generic "people struggle with")
- [ ] Prospect cannot "sleep tonight" because of this problem (demands action NOW)

**Score: ___/6**

### 1B: Unique Promise

- [ ] Promise passes the Logo Test (would BREAK if competitor's name were substituted)
- [ ] Promise is tied to a proprietary MECHANISM (not just a bigger claim)
- [ ] Promise is QUANTIFIED where possible (timeframe, degree, measurable outcome)
- [ ] Promise is BELIEVABLE on its face (not so extraordinary it triggers skepticism)
- [ ] Promise is stated or implied within the first 20% of copy
- [ ] Promise makes THIS solution the ONLY logical choice (not "better" -- DIFFERENT)

**Score: ___/6**

### 1C: Unquestionable Proof

- [ ] Minimum 3 proof TYPES are stacked (from: demonstration, specifics, expert, testimonial, logic, analogy)
- [ ] Proof Sandwich present: moderate claim + proof → bigger claim + proof → biggest claim + proof
- [ ] Proof is placed ADJACENT to every major claim (no distance between claim and evidence)
- [ ] All three Pillars of Conviction present (see Section 3)
- [ ] Proof is SPECIFIC: names, numbers, institutions, dates (no "studies show" without naming the study)
- [ ] Proof stacked so high that doubt becomes IRRATIONAL

**Score: ___/6**

### 1D: Irresistible Proposition

- [ ] Offer has ZERO unnecessary friction (every barrier identified and removed)
- [ ] Guarantee is bold enough to function as a CONVICTION DEVICE
- [ ] First step is framed as "try" not "buy" (low perceived commitment)
- [ ] Risk reversal is clearly stated and feels generous
- [ ] Price is justified by mechanism value (not by discounting)
- [ ] Saying NO feels stupid (value stack + risk reversal + bonuses)

**Score: ___/6**

**Equation Ceiling Test:** Take the LOWEST component score. Multiply by 4. That is your ceiling. A 6/6/6/2 caps at 8/24. The weakest component LIMITS the whole.

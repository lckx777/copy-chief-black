## 1. Emotional Selling Proposition (ESP) Audit

The ESP is the emotional backbone of the entire promotion. Without it,
the copy may inform but will not convert. This section validates that
the emotional architecture is sound and consistent throughout.

### 1A: ESP Foundation

| # | Check | Pass? | Why It Matters |
|---|-------|-------|----------------|
| 1 | [ ] **DRE Identified** -- One dominant emotion is explicitly identified and documented (Fear, Anger, Shame, Frustration, Greed, Hope, etc. from the 16 Emotions Inventory). | | Without a named DRE, the emotional thread will drift. Makepeace never wrote a word without knowing which emotion was driving the sale. |
| 2 | [ ] **DRE Sourced from VOC** -- The dominant emotion was identified from actual prospect language (forum posts, reviews, interviews, comments), NOT assumed by the copywriter. | | Assumed emotions miss the mark 70% of the time. The prospect's OWN WORDS reveal their DRE -- nothing else is reliable. |
| 3 | [ ] **ESP Statement Written** -- A one-sentence ESP statement exists: "This copy makes the reader feel [SPECIFIC EMOTION] about [SPECIFIC PROBLEM] so intensely that buying is the only relief." | | If you cannot articulate the ESP in one sentence, you don't have one. Vague emotional intent produces vague emotional copy. |
| 4 | [ ] **ESP Distinct from USP** -- The ESP is NOT the same as the USP. The USP is the logical differentiator. The ESP is the emotional differentiator. Both exist and are different statements. | | Confusing USP with ESP produces intellectualized copy that engages the brain without moving the gut. |
| 5 | [ ] **Secondary Emotions Identified** -- 2-4 secondary emotions are listed as supporting the DRE, creating emotional variety throughout the copy. | | A single emotion sustained for 3,000+ words creates fatigue. Secondary emotions provide the contrast that keeps the DRE fresh. |
| 6 | [ ] **Emotion Escalation Mapped** -- The emotional intensity increases from lead to close. The weakest emotional moment is the opening (paradoxically -- it starts strong and gets STRONGER). The strongest is the close. | | Flat emotional arcs lose readers. The copy must build like a pressure cooker toward the CTA. |

**Score: ___/6 (Minimum: 5/6)**

## 2. Power Lead Validation

The lead is the single most important element in the promotion.
Makepeace wrote 5+ leads for every letter and picked the one with
the most raw energy. This section validates lead quality.

### 2A: Lead Type and Selection

| # | Check | Pass? | Why It Matters |
|---|-------|-------|----------------|
| 1 | [ ] **Lead type identified** -- The lead matches one of Makepeace's 6 types: Shock, Story, Prophecy, Question, Contrast, or Secret. | | Random leads produce random results. Each type has a specific psychological mechanism that must match the audience. |
| 2 | [ ] **Lead type matches awareness level** -- Shock/Question for Problem-Aware. Story/Secret for Unaware. Contrast/Prophecy for Solution-Aware. (See makepeace.md Lead Selection Matrix.) | | Wrong lead type for wrong awareness level = wasted energy. A Shock Lead on an Unaware audience confuses. A Story Lead on a Most-Aware audience bores. |
| 3 | [ ] **Multiple leads written** -- At least 3 different lead variations were written before selecting the final one. | | The first lead you write is rarely the best. The best lead often comes from attempt 3, 4, or 5. |
| 4 | [ ] **Read-aloud test passed** -- The lead was read aloud. No stumbling. No loss of energy. Each sentence pulls into the next. | | If YOU stumble reading it, the prospect will quit reading entirely. |

**Score: ___/4 (Minimum: 3/4)**

### 2B: Lead Quality (First 500 Words)

| # | Check | Pass? | Why It Matters |
|---|-------|-------|----------------|
| 1 | [ ] **Emotional impact in sentence 1** -- The very first sentence creates an emotional reaction: surprise, fear, curiosity, outrage, or desire. NOT information delivery. | | You have 3 seconds. One sentence. If it doesn't HIT, the reader is gone. Information can wait. Emotion cannot. |
| 2 | [ ] **No warm-up paragraphs** -- Zero throat-clearing. No "In today's world..." No "Dear Friend, I'm writing to you because..." The lead opens with IMPACT. | | Every word before the first emotional impact is a word that loses readers. Cut ruthlessly. |
| 3 | [ ] **Specific detail in first 3 sentences** -- At least one concrete element: a non-round number, a named person, a named place, a specific date. | | Specificity in the first 3 sentences signals "this is REAL, not marketing." It lowers the prospect's hype-detector immediately. |
| 4 | [ ] **No credentials in first 5 sentences** -- The lead does NOT open with who the expert is, what their degrees are, or what company this is. | | Nobody cares who you are until they care ABOUT WHAT you're saying. Credentials are earned after interest is established. |
| 5 | [ ] **Sensory language present** -- At least 2 instances of sensory detail: sounds, sights, physical sensations, textures, temperatures. | | Sensory language activates the reader's body, not just their brain. A reader who FEELS the copy is 10x more likely to continue reading. |
| 6 | [ ] **Emotional tension UNRESOLVED** -- The lead opens a loop that is NOT closed within the lead itself. The reader must continue reading to resolve the tension. | | A lead that resolves its own tension gives the reader permission to stop. The tension must PULL them forward, not release them. |
| 7 | [ ] **Under 500 words** -- The lead does not exceed 500 words before transitioning to the body. | | A lead that runs too long loses its energy. The lead is a SPRINT, not a marathon. |
| 8 | [ ] **Promise present (implicit or explicit)** -- Within the lead, the reader understands what they will GAIN by continuing to read. | | Curiosity alone wears off. A promise sustains attention. The reader needs to know: "What's in it for me if I keep reading?" |

**Score: ___/8 (Minimum: 7/8)**

| # | Check | Pass? | Why It Matters |
|---|-------|-------|----------------|
| 1 | [ ] **No proof desert** -- No section of 200+ words without at least one proof element. | | The reader's skeptic brain activates after ~200 words of unproven claims. Maximum 200 words between proof anchors. |
| 2 | [ ] **Proof adjacent to claims** -- Every major claim has its proof within 2-3 sentences (not in a separate section). | | Distance between claim and proof allows doubt to grow. Adjacency kills doubt instantly. |
| 3 | [ ] **Proof sequence correct** -- Mechanism proof appears first, followed by expert, then demonstration, then testimonial. | | This mirrors the brain's natural processing: "How does it work?" -> "Who says so?" -> "What are the results?" -> "Who has done it?" |

## 4. Fascination Quality Scoring

Each fascination bullet must be individually scored. Then the
full stack is scored as a set. This is the most granular section
of the checklist because fascinations are the ENGINE of response.

### 4A: Individual Fascination Test (Run Per Bullet)

| # | Check | Pass? | Why It Matters |
|---|-------|-------|----------------|
| 1 | [ ] **Information Gap Created** -- The fascination hints at something specific but withholds the KEY reveal. The reader MUST buy to satisfy the curiosity. | | A fascination that reveals its own answer has zero selling power. The gap IS the sale. |
| 2 | [ ] **Standalone Desire** -- This single fascination creates enough desire to justify purchasing the ENTIRE product. "Would someone buy for THIS ALONE?" | | Makepeace gold standard. Every fascination is a mini-ad for the whole product. |
| 3 | [ ] **Specific Number Present** -- Contains at least one non-round number (87%, $4, 19 days, 2,847 participants). | | Numbers signal research, precision, and reality. Round numbers signal estimation and invention. |
| 4 | [ ] **Named Element** -- Contains a named person, place, institution, or specific identifier (clinical trial #, page reference). | | Anonymous fascinations feel generic. Named fascinations feel discovered. |
| 5 | [ ] **Page/Section Reference** -- References a specific page, chapter, or section (e.g., "page 47"). | | Page references imply a REAL product with real substance. They make the invisible tangible. |
| 6 | [ ] **Emotional Charge** -- The fascination activates at least one emotion: fear, curiosity, greed, outrage, hope, vindication. You can NAME which one. | | A fascination running on pure information without emotional charge is trivia, not selling. |
| 7 | [ ] **Under 3 Lines** -- The fascination is tight, compressed, punchy. No more than 3 lines of text. | | Long fascinations lose their punch. A fascination is a jab. The moment it becomes a paragraph, it stops being a fascination. |
| 8 | [ ] **No Feature Language** -- Zero instances of "Includes...", "Learn about...", "Information on...", "Tips for...", "A chapter covering..." | | These structures DESCRIBE the product. Fascinations SELL the product. Table of contents entries do not create desire. |
| 9 | [ ] **Type Identified** -- The fascination matches one of the 21 Types (Naked Benefit, Transactional, Inverted, Curiosity Teaser, If-Then, Truth About, Why, What, What Never, Number, Right/Wrong, How To, Secret, Warning, Quickest/Easiest, Specific Mechanism, Contrarian, Insider, Time-Bound, Comparison, PLUS). | | Knowing the type ensures intentional variety and prevents monotony. |
| 10 | [ ] **Logo Test** -- No competitor could use this fascination without modification. It contains details proprietary to THIS offer. | | Generic fascinations are interchangeable. Makepeace-grade fascinations are anchored to specific mechanisms, names, and stories that belong to ONE offer. |

**Individual Scoring:**

| Score | Rating | Action |
|-------|--------|--------|
| 9-10 | MAKEPEACE-GRADE | Ship with confidence |
| 7-8 | STRONG | Minor tightening, then ship |
| 5-6 | ADEQUATE | Rewrite -- missing key elements |
| 3-4 | WEAK | Major rewrite required |
| Below 3 | REJECT | Scrap and write new fascination |

### 4B: Fascination Stack Test (Full Set)

| # | Check | Pass? | Why It Matters |
|---|-------|-------|----------------|
| S1 | [ ] **Minimum 15 fascinations** -- Long-form sales letter has at least 15 fascinations total (across bullets + embedded). | | Fewer than 15 does not create the cumulative "desire avalanche" that Makepeace promos generate. |
| S2 | [ ] **Minimum 8 different types used** -- The stack uses at least 8 of the 21 fascination types. | | Same-type fascinations create monotony. Variety keeps each bullet fresh and activates different psychological levers. |
| S3 | [ ] **Strongest fascination FIRST** -- The single most compelling fascination opens the stack (primacy effect). | | The first bullet determines whether the reader commits to reading the rest. A weak opener loses them. |
| S4 | [ ] **Second-strongest LAST** -- The second most compelling fascination closes the stack (recency effect). | | The last bullet determines the emotional state when the reader hits the offer. Make it powerful. |
| S5 | [ ] **PLUS bullet at end** -- The final fascination uses the "PLUS" type ("PLUS -- [additional greed-stoking benefit]"). | | The PLUS bullet triggers the greed cascade: more-more-MORE. It pushes the reader over the edge. |
| S6 | [ ] **No consecutive same-type** -- No two adjacent fascinations use the same type. | | Adjacent same-type fascinations feel repetitive even if the content differs. The brain craves pattern variety. |
| S7 | [ ] **Opening word variety** -- No two consecutive fascinations start with the same word or structure. | | "The... The... The..." creates visual monotony. Each fascination must LOOK different at first glance. |
| S8 | [ ] **Paragraph breaks present** -- No more than 4 fascinations without a visual break (white space, sub-head, or transition sentence). | | A wall of bullets overwhelms the eye. Strategic breaks let each cluster breathe and maintain impact. |
| S9 | [ ] **Read-aloud test** -- Every fascination read aloud. Any stumble = rewrite. Any energy drop = rewrite. | | The ear catches what the eye misses. Stumbling kills the rhythm that makes fascination stacks hypnotic. |
| S10 | [ ] **"So What?" test** -- For each fascination: "So what?" If no immediate, visceral answer, add stakes or cut. | | Curiosity without stakes is trivia. Curiosity WITH stakes is urgency. Every fascination must answer "So what?" with "YOUR LIFE depends on knowing this." |

**Stack Score: ___/10 (Minimum: 8/10)**

## 6. Selling the Diagnosis (Health Copy Only)

For health and supplement copy, this is the most CRITICAL section.
Makepeace spent 35-50% of health copy selling the DIAGNOSIS before
ever mentioning the product. Skip this and the sale collapses.

### 6A: Diagnosis Sequence Check

| # | Step | Present? | Word Count | Quality (1-10) |
|---|------|----------|------------|-----------------|
| 1 | [ ] **Problem Validation** -- "You've been suffering from X, and it's real." The prospect's suffering is ACKNOWLEDGED, not minimized. | | _____ | |
| 2 | [ ] **Previous Solution Invalidation** -- "You've tried Y and Z, but they failed because they targeted the WRONG thing." Explains WHY nothing has worked. | | _____ | |
| 3 | [ ] **Real Cause Revelation** -- "The REAL reason is [new mechanism]." A paradigm shift. Something the prospect has NEVER heard before. | | _____ | |
| 4 | [ ] **Mechanism Naming** -- The cause has a PROPRIETARY NAME -- memorable, intriguing, slightly scientific but accessible. | | _____ | |
| 5 | [ ] **Mechanism Explanation** -- HOW the mechanism works, explained with analogy and plain language. An 8-year-old could follow the logic. | | _____ | |
| 6 | [ ] **Exoneration** -- "It's NOT your fault. It's not your genes. It's not your willpower. It's THIS mechanism." The prospect is absolved. | | _____ | |
| 7 | [ ] **Only-Solution Bridge** -- The ONLY way to address THIS specific mechanism is the product being sold. No other approach targets THIS cause. | | _____ | |

**Score: ___/7 (Minimum: 6/7 -- ALL first 6 are non-negotiable)**

### 6B: Diagnosis Quality Checks

| # | Check | Pass? | Why It Matters |
|---|-------|-------|----------------|
| 1 | [ ] **Diagnosis occupies 35-50% of total copy** -- Word count verification. The diagnosis section (Steps 1-6 above) constitutes 35-50% of the entire letter. | | Rushing to the product before fully selling the diagnosis is the #1 amateur mistake in health copy. |
| 2 | [ ] **Mechanism name passes "cocktail party test"** -- Would a prospect REMEMBER the mechanism name and TELL a friend about it at a party? | | A forgettable mechanism name produces forgettable copy. The name must stick like gum to a shoe. |
| 3 | [ ] **Analogy present for complex biology** -- At least one "It's like [familiar thing]" analogy to make the mechanism tangible. | | Complex science without analogy creates confusion. Analogy creates the "aha moment" that makes the sale. |
| 4 | [ ] **Enemy identified** -- There is a VILLAIN: Big Pharma, conventional doctors, the food industry, a specific company, a specific policy. | | Enemies give the prospect someone to blame AND a reason to trust YOUR alternative. Without an enemy, the copy lacks anger and vindication energy. |
| 5 | [ ] **"Why nothing worked" is answered** -- The copy explicitly explains why every previous solution the prospect tried was doomed to fail. | | If the prospect doesn't understand WHY everything else failed, they won't believe THIS solution is different. |
| 6 | [ ] **"Aha moment" present** -- There is a single sentence or short paragraph where the reader goes "THAT'S why!" -- the moment the diagnosis clicks. | | The "aha moment" is the sale. It is the instant the prospect's paradigm shifts and your product becomes the only logical answer. |

**Score: ___/6 (Minimum: 5/6)**

## 7. Lead Type Selection Guide

Use this guide to select the optimal lead type BEFORE writing. Match
the lead type to the audience's awareness level and the market's
sophistication stage.

### 7A: Audience Analysis (Complete Before Writing)

| Question | Answer |
|----------|--------|
| **Awareness Level:** Unaware / Problem-Aware / Solution-Aware / Product-Aware / Most Aware | _________ |
| **Sophistication Level:** 1 (First to Market) / 2 (Enlarged Claim) / 3 (Mechanism) / 4 (Mechanism++) / 5 (Identification) | _________ |
| **Primary DRE:** Which of the 16 emotions dominates? | _________ |
| **Previous solutions tried:** What has the prospect already attempted? | _________ |
| **Market temperature:** Cold (doesn't know the market exists) / Warm (shops actively) / Hot (ready to buy today) | _________ |

### 7B: Lead Type Recommendation Matrix

| Awareness | Sophistication | Market Temp | Recommended Lead Types | Notes |
|-----------|---------------|-------------|----------------------|-------|
| Unaware | 1-2 | Cold | Story, Secret | Must enter through narrative |
| Problem-Aware | 2-3 | Warm | Shock, Question | Amplify existing pain |
| Solution-Aware | 3-4 | Warm | Contrast, Prophecy | Show THIS solution is different |
| Product-Aware | 4-5 | Hot | Shock, Prophecy | Reframe what they think they know |
| Most Aware | 5 | Hot | Question, Contrast | Short lead, straight to offer |

### 7C: Lead Type Quality Checks (Type-Specific)

**If using SHOCK Lead:**
- [ ] Opening fact/statistic is SPECIFIC (non-round number)
- [ ] Fact is emotionally DISTURBING (not just interesting)
- [ ] Source/credibility for the fact is provided within 3 sentences
- [ ] Transition from shock to "what this means for YOU"

**If using STORY Lead:**
- [ ] Opens in medias res (middle of the action, not the beginning)
- [ ] Named character with age, occupation, location
- [ ] Sensory details (at least 2: sound, sight, touch, smell)
- [ ] Story connects to the reader's situation within 200 words

**If using PROPHECY Lead:**
- [ ] Specific date or timeframe for the prediction
- [ ] Credible basis for the prediction (not just assertion)
- [ ] Historical pattern cited as evidence (dates and data)
- [ ] Stakes made personal: what happens to the READER if this comes true

**If using QUESTION Lead:**
- [ ] Question targets a known pain point (not generic)
- [ ] Prospect CANNOT answer "no" honestly (the question is rigged)
- [ ] Question implies the existence of a solution
- [ ] Follow-up sentences deepen the question, don't answer it

**If using CONTRAST Lead:**
- [ ] "Before" world is VIVID and specific (sensory details)
- [ ] "After" world is equally vivid and specific
- [ ] Gap between before/after creates desire
- [ ] The BRIDGE from before to after is hinted at but not revealed

**If using SECRET Lead:**
- [ ] The "secret" is framed as DISCOVERED (not invented)
- [ ] Source of discovery is named (researcher, institution, place)
- [ ] The secret promises a specific, measurable benefit
- [ ] Exclusivity is implied (few people know this)

## 9. Urgency Architecture Check

Urgency must be ARCHITECTURAL -- woven throughout the copy from
paragraph one, not tacked on at the end. This section validates
all three urgency layers.

| # | Check | Pass? | Why It Matters |
|---|-------|-------|----------------|
| 1 | [ ] **Consequence urgency in body** -- The cost of INACTION is made vivid and specific throughout the body copy. Not just "act now" but "every day you wait, X gets worse." | | Consequence urgency is the most powerful form because it is organic -- it comes from the problem itself, not an artificial deadline. |
| 2 | [ ] **Consequence urgency is SPECIFIC** -- Not "things will get worse" but "every day, 3 more hair cells die and they NEVER regenerate." With a specific mechanism. | | Vague consequences produce vague urgency. Specific consequences create visceral "I need to act NOW" responses. |
| 3 | [ ] **Scarcity urgency in offer** -- Limited supply, limited batch, limited enrollment. With SPECIFIC numbers (not "limited quantities"). | | "Limited quantities" is cliche. "We produced 2,000 bottles and 14,000 people will read this letter" is credible scarcity. |
| 4 | [ ] **Opportunity urgency in close** -- A specific window, deadline, or event that creates time pressure. | | Open-ended offers produce open-ended procrastination. A window that CLOSES creates immediate action. |
| 5 | [ ] **All 3 urgency types present** -- Consequence (body), Scarcity (offer), Opportunity (close). Layered, not scattered. | | Single-type urgency can be dismissed. Triple-layered urgency creates psychological inevitability. |
| 6 | [ ] **Urgency feels ORGANIC** -- The urgency arises naturally from the situation described, not from manufactured pressure. | | Artificial urgency ("ACT NOW! LIMITED TIME!") triggers hype-resistance. Organic urgency ("This batch expires; the clinical trial ends; the damage compounds daily") triggers genuine action. |
| 7 | [ ] **PS contains urgency** -- At least one PS line restates the urgency with a specific deadline or consequence. | | Many readers skip to the PS first. If urgency isn't there, they feel no pressure when they encounter the CTA. |

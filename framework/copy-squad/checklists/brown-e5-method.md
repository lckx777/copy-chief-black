## E5 Phase Checklist (What MUST Be in Each Phase)

### E1: EXPOSE / EMOTION (25-30% of copy)

**Mandatory Elements:**
- [ ] Opening mirrors prospect's CURRENT emotional state (not desired state)
- [ ] Problem is REFRAMED (not restated -- must be genuinely new angle)
- [ ] New cause identified and NAMED (Sexy Cause)
- [ ] Prospect's current understanding proven INCOMPLETE (not wrong)
- [ ] Old belief validated FIRST, then complicated ("You were right to try X...")
- [ ] Blame removed from prospect (external cause established)
- [ ] Failed solutions explained through new lens ("THAT'S why X never worked")
- [ ] Emotional stakes escalated (from immediate discomfort to long-term consequence)
- [ ] PIVOT moment present ("But what if the problem isn't what you think?")
- [ ] Allocation check: E1 is 25-30% of total copy (not under 20%)

**E1 Quality Questions:**
- "Does this REFRAME what they think the problem is?" [ ]
- "Would they say 'I never thought of it that way'?" [ ]
- "Does it explain why everything they tried FAILED?" [ ]
- "Does the prospect feel UNDERSTOOD (not sold to)?" [ ]
- "Is the emotion FELT before any mechanism is mentioned?" [ ]

**E1 Tone by Niche:**
| Niche | Tone | Opens With |
|-------|------|-----------|
| Saude | Clinical empathy | "You know that feeling at 3AM when..." |
| Concursos | Frustrated solidarity | "You've studied 6 hours a day for 14 months..." |
| Relacionamento | Quiet desperation | "You lay in bed next to someone who feels like..." |
| Riqueza | Simmering resentment | "You watch people half as smart as you..." |

### E2: ELEVATE / EDUCATION (20-25% of copy)

**Mandatory Elements:**
- [ ] MUP reveal present: "The REAL reason [problem] persists is [mechanism name]"
- [ ] Mechanism is PROPRIETARY (no competitor can claim it)
- [ ] Mechanism has a SPECIFIC, STICKY NAME (Gimmick Name)
- [ ] Mechanism is grounded in something REAL (anatomy, psychology, data, process)
- [ ] Mechanism is VISUAL -- prospect can SEE it in their mind (analogy used)
- [ ] Why conventional solutions fail is explicitly explained through mechanism
- [ ] "Aha moment" present (point where understanding reorganizes)
- [ ] Mechanism makes all alternatives look INCOMPLETE (not just "worse")
- [ ] Revelation sequence used: Tease > Context > Reveal > Validate > Connect
- [ ] Allocation check: E2 is 20-25% of total copy (this is THE core)

**Mechanism 5 Functions (ALL Required):**

| Function | Present? | How? |
|----------|----------|------|
| 1. EXPLAINS why problem exists | [ ] | ___ |
| 2. ABSOLVES prospect of blame | [ ] | ___ |
| 3. INVALIDATES other solutions | [ ] | ___ |
| 4. POSITIONS your solution uniquely | [ ] | ___ |
| 5. URGENTIZES action | [ ] | ___ |

**E2 Quality Questions:**
- "Can the prospect explain the mechanism to a friend using YOUR language?" [ ]
- "Does the mechanism have a visual analogy (piano keys, crystals, etc.)?" [ ]
- "Is the 'How I Discovered...' narrative used (story > lecture)?" [ ]

### E3: EXCITE / EVIDENCE (15-20% of copy)

**Mandatory Elements:**
- [ ] Minimum 3 proof types stacked (see table below)
- [ ] Evidence serves the MECHANISM (not just the product)
- [ ] Evidence is SPECIFIC (study names, years, sample sizes, expert names)
- [ ] Proof escalation: small claims with small proof FIRST, bigger claims later

**Evidence Stack (ordered by presentation sequence):**

| # | Proof Type | Present? | Specific? | Details |
|---|------------|----------|-----------|---------|
| 1 | Scientific/Research | [ ] | [ ] | Study: ___ Year: ___ Finding: ___ |
| 2 | Expert Endorsement | [ ] | [ ] | Expert: ___ Credential: ___ Quote: ___ |
| 3 | Logical Demonstration | [ ] | [ ] | If [mechanism] then [result] because: ___ |
| 4 | Social Proof/Testimonials | [ ] | [ ] | Name: ___ Detail: ___ Result: ___ |
| 5 | Visual/Demo | [ ] | [ ] | What they see: ___ |

**E3 Quality Question:**
- "Take a skeptical person. Read ONLY E3. Do they think 'this might be real'?" [ ]

### E4: EMPOWER / EXCITEMENT (15-20% of copy)

**Mandatory Elements:**
- [ ] Focus on VISION, not features
- [ ] Future state described with VIVID SENSORY detail (sight, sound, touch, smell)
- [ ] Involvement devices used ("imagine," "picture this," "feel what it's like")
- [ ] Specific scenarios described (not vague promises)
- [ ] Contrast with current state ("Instead of [pain], you [gain]")
- [ ] Social proof WITHIN the future-pacing ("Just like [name] who [result]")
- [ ] Emotional peak created (hope, excitement, relief)
- [ ] Result feels ACHIEVABLE, not fantasy
- [ ] Connected back to mechanism: "This is possible BECAUSE of [mechanism]"

**E4 Quality Question:**
- "Does the prospect feel a visceral PULL (not just 'that sounds nice')?" [ ]

### E5: EARN / ENGAGEMENT (10-15% of copy)

**Mandatory Elements:**
- [ ] Restatement of mechanism's value (not product features)
- [ ] Offer feels INEVITABLE given E1-E4 (not forced pivot)
- [ ] Every stack component connects to mechanism (no filler bonuses)
- [ ] Price anchored against cost of NOT solving (not competitor pricing)
- [ ] Risk reversal is MECHANISM-SPECIFIC ("If [mechanism] doesn't [result]...")
- [ ] Urgency based on mechanism logic ("Every day without addressing [mechanism]...")
- [ ] CTA is natural conclusion, not hard sell
- [ ] Price justified by mechanism value (not discount)
- [ ] Allocation check: E5 is 10-15%. If more, E1-E4 didn't do enough.

**E5 Quality Question:**
- "Does the prospect feel 'I'd be stupid NOT to' (not 'I guess I could try')?" [ ]

## Unique Mechanism Audit (Todd Brown's 7-Point Test)

**Run this BEFORE writing any copy. If mechanism fails, fix mechanism first.**

| # | Test | Question | Pass? | Notes |
|---|------|----------|-------|-------|
| 1 | **Novelty** | Is this truly NEW to this market? | [ ] | ___ |
| 2 | **Proprietary Name** | Does it have a name only YOU can claim? | [ ] | ___ |
| 3 | **60-Second Explain** | Can you explain it clearly in under 60 seconds? | [ ] | ___ |
| 4 | **Failure Explanation** | Does it make past failures make sense? | [ ] | ___ |
| 5 | **Blame Removal** | Does it remove blame from the prospect? | [ ] | ___ |
| 6 | **Category of One** | Does it create a category where you're the only player? | [ ] | ___ |
| 7 | **Origin Story** | Is there a credible story of HOW it was discovered? | [ ] | ___ |

**Audit Score: ___/7**

| Score | Decision |
|-------|----------|
| 7/7 | STRONG -- Ready for copy production |
| 5-6/7 | ADEQUATE -- Strengthen weak points before production |
| Below 5/7 | WEAK -- Return to mechanism development. Do NOT write copy. |

**If Failing, Common Fixes:**

| Fails | Fix |
|-------|-----|
| #1 (Novelty) | Dig deeper into research. Find sub-mechanism that IS new. |
| #2 (Name) | Create branded wrapper. "Otolith Crystals" not "calcium deposits." |
| #3 (Explain) | Simplify to ONE core concept. Use analogy. |
| #4 (Failures) | Rethink MUP. Mechanism MUST make failures logical. |
| #5 (Blame) | Shift to external cause: biology, hidden system, environment. |
| #6 (Category) | Differentiate at sub-mechanism level or find different angle. |
| #7 (Origin) | Research actual history. If invented, create plausible provenance. |

## "Category of One" Positioning Test

This test validates that your mechanism creates a genuine new category
where you are the ONLY player, not just the "best" player.

### Category Identification

Current market category your offer would normally be placed in: ___
(e.g., "weight loss supplement," "exam prep course," "tinnitus treatment")

### 5-Point Category of One Test

| # | Test | Question | Result |
|---|------|----------|--------|
| 1 | **No Comparison** | Can the prospect compare you to ANY existing offer? | [ ] NO (pass) / [ ] YES (fail) |
| 2 | **New Language** | Does your marketing use DIFFERENT vocabulary than the category? | [ ] YES (pass) / [ ] NO (fail) |
| 3 | **Mechanism Exclusivity** | Is your mechanism something NO competitor addresses? | [ ] YES (pass) / [ ] NO (fail) |
| 4 | **Category Escape** | Can you describe the offer WITHOUT referencing the old category? | [ ] YES (pass) / [ ] NO (fail) |
| 5 | **Google Test** | If prospect Googles your mechanism name, do they find ONLY you? | [ ] YES (pass) / [ ] NO (fail) |

**Score: ___/5**

| Score | Assessment |
|-------|-----------|
| 5/5 | TRUE Category of One -- proceed |
| 3-4/5 | Partial differentiation -- strengthen weak points |
| Below 3/5 | Still in existing category -- rethink mechanism positioning |

### Category Reframe Check

| Check | Status |
|-------|--------|
| Do you EVER say "better than" or "unlike other [category]"? | [ ] NO (good) / [ ] YES (fix) |
| Do you EVER use existing category labels? ("our supplement," "our course") | [ ] NO (good) / [ ] YES (fix) |
| Do you reference competitors by name? | [ ] NO (good) / [ ] YES (fix) |
| Is your mechanism the REASON for the new category? | [ ] YES (good) / [ ] NO (fix) |

## Paradigm Shift Check

| Step | Present? | Content |
|------|----------|---------|
| CURRENT PARADIGM | [ ] | "They currently believe ___" |
| DISRUPTION | [ ] | "The new information: ___" |
| NEW PARADIGM | [ ] | "Now they believe ___" |
| BRIDGE TO SOLUTION | [ ] | "Therefore they must ___" |

**Quality Checks:**
- [ ] Old belief is SPECIFIC (not a straw man)
- [ ] Old belief is VALIDATED first ("You were right to think...")
- [ ] Disruption feels like REVELATION (not argument or lecture)
- [ ] New belief feels INEVITABLE after disruption
- [ ] New belief explains PAST FAILURES (retroactive sense-making)
- [ ] Bridge leads naturally to your product as THE only response
- [ ] Prospect's intelligence is not attacked
- [ ] Shift feels like DISCOVERY, not like being sold

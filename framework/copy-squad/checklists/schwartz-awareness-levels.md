## Part 1: Awareness Level Diagnostic

### How to Identify Which Level Your Market Is At

Answer the following questions using VOC data, not assumptions.
Each "Yes" moves you to a higher awareness level. Stop at the
first "No" -- that is your prospect's awareness level.

| # | Diagnostic Question | Data Source | If YES | If NO |
|---|---------------------|-------------|--------|-------|
| 1 | Does the prospect know they have THIS specific problem? | VOC: complaints, forum posts, search queries about symptoms | Proceed to Q2 | **LEVEL 5: UNAWARE** |
| 2 | Has the prospect actively searched for solutions to this problem? | VOC: review sites, comparison queries, "best X for Y" searches | Proceed to Q3 | **LEVEL 4: PROBLEM AWARE** |
| 3 | Has the prospect heard of YOUR specific product/brand? | VOC: brand mentions, direct search for product name, retargeting data | Proceed to Q4 | **LEVEL 3: SOLUTION AWARE** |
| 4 | Has the prospect evaluated your product but NOT purchased? | VOC: cart abandonment, "is X worth it?" queries, comparison with competitors | Proceed to Q5 | **LEVEL 2: PRODUCT AWARE** |
| 5 | Is the prospect ready to buy RIGHT NOW if given the right deal? | VOC: "discount code for X", "when does X go on sale?", repeat visitors to checkout | YES | **LEVEL 1: MOST AWARE** |

### VOC Signals by Level

| Level | What They Say (VOC) | Where to Find It |
|-------|---------------------|------------------|
| **Unaware** | No direct signals. They are NOT searching. | Inferred from adjacent interests, demographics, behavioral data |
| **Problem Aware** | "Why does [symptom] happen?", "Is this normal?", "Am I the only one who...", "I'm so frustrated with [problem]" | Health forums, Reddit, support groups, social media comments |
| **Solution Aware** | "What's the best [solution type]?", "Which [solution] actually works?", "I've tried X and Y but...", "Has anyone found something that..." | Comparison sites, review platforms, "best of" search queries |
| **Product Aware** | "Has anyone tried [product]?", "Is [product] worth it?", "[Product] vs [competitor]", "Does [product] really work?" | Product reviews, competitor comparisons, retargeting audiences |
| **Most Aware** | "Where to buy [product]?", "Is there a discount?", "When does the sale end?", "Coupon code for [product]" | Direct search, email list, retargeted visitors, brand social followers |

### Quick Decision Tree

```
Does the prospect KNOW about their problem?
├── NO → UNAWARE (Level 5)
│   Headline: Story/Curiosity. Zero product mention.
│
└── YES → Do they know SOLUTIONS exist?
    ├── NO → PROBLEM AWARE (Level 4)
    │   Headline: Name symptom. Empathize. Hint at answer.
    │
    └── YES → Do they know YOUR product?
        ├── NO → SOLUTION AWARE (Level 3)
        │   Headline: Mechanism. Why THIS approach is different.
        │
        └── YES → Have they DECIDED to buy?
            ├── NO → PRODUCT AWARE (Level 2)
            │   Headline: Proof. Overcome their specific objection.
            │
            └── YES → MOST AWARE (Level 1)
                Headline: Deal. Offer. Get out of the way.
```

## Part 2: Sophistication Level Diagnostic

### How to Identify Market Sophistication

Sophistication is about EXPOSURE to marketing claims, NOT
intelligence. Answer these questions by analyzing the competitive
landscape, not the prospect's education level.

| # | Diagnostic Question | Data Source | If YES | If NO |
|---|---------------------|-------------|--------|-------|
| 1 | Are there OTHER advertisers making similar promises in this market? | Ads Library Spy, competitor analysis | Proceed to Q2 | **LEVEL 1: FIRST** (Make the claim directly) |
| 2 | Are competitors making BIGGER versions of the same claim? | Competitor ads, LP analysis | Proceed to Q3 | **LEVEL 2: SECOND** (Enlarge YOUR claim) |
| 3 | Are competitors using MECHANISMS (named methods, processes, ingredients) in their headlines? | Competitor ads and VSLs | Proceed to Q4 | **LEVEL 3: THIRD** (Introduce YOUR mechanism) |
| 4 | Are those mechanisms FAMILIAR to the market? Has the market seen mechanism-based claims before? | VOC: "I've tried [method] and it didn't work", competitor mechanism names in comments | Proceed to Q5 | **LEVEL 4: FOURTH** (Intensify YOUR mechanism) |
| 5 | Does the market actively DISTRUST marketing? Do they roll their eyes at claims AND mechanisms? | VOC: "It's all a scam", "Another miracle cure", cynical comments on ads | YES = **LEVEL 5: FIFTH** | NO = **LEVEL 4: FOURTH** |

### Sophistication Signals

| Level | Market Signals | Ad Landscape | VOC Signals |
|-------|---------------|--------------|-------------|
| **First** | No competition. Virgin market. | Few or no ads for this category | "I've never seen anything like this" |
| **Second** | Early competition. Claims getting bigger. | Multiple ads with similar promises | "This one promises more than the other" |
| **Third** | Claims exhausted. Mechanisms emerging. | Ads lead with methods/ingredients/processes | "HOW does it work?", "What makes it different?" |
| **Fourth** | Mechanisms common. Specificity war. | Ads use authority, studies, specific numbers | "I've heard that before", "Where's the proof?" |
| **Fifth** | Total marketing fatigue. Narrative wins. | Best-performing ads are story-driven, personal | "I don't trust any of them", "Another miracle cure..." |

### Channel-Specific Sophistication

IMPORTANT: The same market can be at DIFFERENT sophistication
levels on different channels. Map each channel separately.

| Channel | Typical Sophistication | Why |
|---------|----------------------|-----|
| Cold traffic (Meta/Google) | HIGHER (4-5) | Exposed to the most ads |
| Email list (warm) | LOWER (2-3) | Already opted in, pre-trust |
| Retargeting | LOWER (3-4) | Already showed interest |
| Organic social | VARIES | Depends on content exposure |
| Referral traffic | LOWEST (1-2) | Trust transferred from referrer |

## Part 3: Headline Formula Per Awareness Level

### Fill-In Templates

**UNAWARE (Awareness 1) -- Story/Curiosity**

```
Template A (Character Story):
"[Specific character with relatable trait] [unexpected action].
[Shocking/curious consequence]. [Implied benefit hook]."

Fill-in:
"[A/An] [age]-year-old [occupation] from [place] [did unexpected thing].
[What happened next]. [Implied connection to reader's situation]."

Example:
"A 71-year-old German pharmacist could hear a pin drop across the room.
His doctor had no explanation. Then he showed her what was in his cabinet."
```

```
Template B (Discovery Story):
"They found [shocking discovery] in [unexpected location].
[Why it matters to you]."

Fill-in:
"They found [specific thing] in [specific place].
What it means for [target audience] could change everything about [relevant topic]."

Example:
"They found microscopic crystals crushing nerve cells inside the human ear.
What it means for the 50 million Americans with ringing ears could change everything."
```

```
Template C (Pattern Interrupt):
"[Contrarian or unexpected statement that stops the scroll].
[Bridge to relevance]."

Fill-in:
"[Statement that contradicts common belief about familiar topic].
[Why this matters specifically for reader]."

Example:
"Your ears are not ringing because of damage. They're ringing because of crystals."
```

**PROBLEM AWARE (Awareness 2) -- Symptom + Explanation**

```
Template A (If-Then):
"If you [specific symptom], here's [what/why]."

Fill-in:
"If you [experience specific symptom] [at specific time/frequency],
here's [the real reason / what your doctor isn't telling you / why it's getting worse]."

Example:
"If you wake up at 3AM with a high-pitched ringing that wasn't there a year ago,
here's why it's getting louder -- and it has nothing to do with hearing loss."
```

```
Template B (Why-Pattern):
"Why [unexpected thing happens] (and what to do about it)"

Fill-in:
"Why [specific symptom happens] [despite doing expected thing]
(and the [number]-[unit] [action] that [reverses/fixes/stops] it)"

Example:
"Why your tinnitus gets WORSE in silence (and the 3-minute 'crystal dissolution'
technique that European doctors now recommend)"
```

```
Template C (Frustration Mirror):
"Tired of [failed approach]? The problem isn't [what they think]. It's [real cause]."

Fill-in:
"Tired of [specific failed solution they've tried]?
The problem isn't [common assumption]. It's [new mechanism]."

Example:
"Tired of white noise machines that mask the ringing but never stop it?
The problem isn't your ears. It's microscopic crystals crushing your auditory nerves."
```

**SOLUTION AWARE (Awareness 3) -- Mechanism Differentiation**

```
Template A (Mechanism Hero):
"The '[Mechanism Name]' [method/approach/protocol] that [desire] in [timeframe]"

Fill-in:
"The '[proprietary mechanism name]' [method/formula/technique/protocol]
that [specific measurable result] in [specific timeframe]"

Example:
"The 'Otolith Crystal' method that silences tinnitus in 21 days
-- without hearing aids, surgery, or masking devices"
```

```
Template B (Forget-Mechanism):
"Forget [common solution]. [Mechanism] is why [authority] now recommends [this]."

Fill-in:
"Forget [solution they've tried]. [Named mechanism] is why
[specific authority figure/institution] now recommends [new approach]."

Example:
"Forget hearing aids. The 'Crystal Dissolution Protocol' is why
Heidelberg University researchers now recommend this formula instead."
```

```
Template C (Real Reason):
"The real reason [problem persists]. (It's not [what they think]. It's [mechanism].)"

Fill-in:
"The real reason [specific problem won't go away].
(It's not [common assumption]. It's your [named mechanism].)"

Example:
"The real reason your ears won't stop ringing.
(It's not nerve damage. It's Otolith Crystals crushing your cochlear cells.)"
```

**PRODUCT AWARE (Awareness 4) -- Promise + Proof**

```
Template A (Social Proof + Result):
"[Number] [people] have already [used/tried] [product] to [result].
[Specific success metric]."

Fill-in:
"[Non-round number] [demographic] have already [action] [product name]
to [specific result]. [Percentage]% reported [specific outcome] within [timeframe]."

Example:
"12,847 Europeans have already used Neuvelys to silence the ringing in their ears.
87.3% reported significant improvement within 21 days."
```

```
Template B (Product vs Common):
"[Product]: Why it works when [common solutions] don't."

Fill-in:
"[Product name]: Why it works when [solution 1], [solution 2],
and [solution 3] have all failed you."

Example:
"Neuvelys: Why it works when hearing aids, white noise, and supplements have all failed you."
```

**MOST AWARE (Awareness 5) -- Deal Direct**

```
Template A (Discount + Urgency):
"[Product] -- [Discount]% off [this timeframe only]. [Bonus]. [Guarantee]."

Fill-in:
"[Product name] -- [specific discount] [urgency window].
[Specific bonus]. [Specific guarantee]. [CTA]."

Example:
"Neuvelys -- 40% off this week only. 3 bottles for the price of 2.
Free shipping. 180-day guarantee. Order below."
```

## Part 4: Headline Audit Checklist (10 Points)

Run this audit on EVERY headline before it ships. Score each
point 0 (fail) or 1 (pass). Minimum to ship: 8/10.

| # | Audit Point | Question to Ask | Pass/Fail |
|---|-------------|-----------------|-----------|
| 1 | **Awareness Match** | Does this headline match the diagnosed awareness level of the target prospect? Would they recognize themselves in it? | [ ] |
| 2 | **Sophistication Match** | Does this headline match the diagnosed sophistication level of the market? Is it using the right strategic element (claim/mechanism/story)? | [ ] |
| 3 | **Mass Desire Channel** | Does this headline tap into an EXISTING mass desire that thousands already have? Could you trace this desire in VOC data? | [ ] |
| 4 | **One-Level Advance** | Does this headline advance the prospect exactly ONE awareness level? Not two, not three -- exactly one? | [ ] |
| 5 | **Bridge Not Billboard** | Does this headline enter the prospect's EXISTING mental conversation? Or does it broadcast from the brand's perspective? | [ ] |
| 6 | **Specificity** | Does this headline contain at least ONE specific element (non-round number, named mechanism, named authority, specific timeframe, or sensory detail)? | [ ] |
| 7 | **Mechanism Present** | If the market is at Sophistication 3+, does the headline lead with a NAMED mechanism rather than a bare promise? | [ ] |
| 8 | **Logo Test** | Could a competitor use this EXACT headline by swapping in their product name? If yes, it FAILS. | [ ] |
| 9 | **Hidden Desire Activation** | Does this headline (or the first 50 words that follow it) activate the hidden desire through IMPLICATION, never direct statement? | [ ] |
| 10 | **Unstoppable Momentum** | After reading this headline, does the reader NEED to read the next line? Is there an information gap, a promise, or a hook that demands continuation? | [ ] |

**SCORE: ___ / 10**

| Score | Rating | Action |
|-------|--------|--------|
| 9-10 | SCHWARTZ-GRADE | Ship with confidence |
| 8 | STRONG | Minor refinement, then ship |
| 6-7 | ADEQUATE | Rewrite using failed checks as guide |
| 4-5 | WEAK | Major rewrite -- likely awareness/sophistication mismatch |
| Below 4 | REJECT | Re-diagnose awareness and sophistication. Start over. |

## Part 5: Mass Desire Identification Guide

### Step-by-Step Process

Before writing a SINGLE word of copy, complete this entire
identification process. Desire that is not identified cannot
be channeled.

**Step 1: Follow the Money**

```
What is the market ALREADY spending money on?
- [ ] List 5 products/services they currently buy: ___
- [ ] What price range are they willing to pay? ___
- [ ] How frequently do they purchase? ___
- [ ] What does their spending pattern reveal about their desire? ___

INSIGHT: Desire without spending is a wish, not a desire.
Real desire has a paper trail.
```

**Step 2: Follow the Complaints**

```
What is the market FRUSTRATED about?
- [ ] List 5 complaints from VOC (with quotes): ___
- [ ] What have they tried that FAILED? ___
- [ ] What do they blame for the failure? ___
- [ ] What emotion dominates the complaints? (Frustration/Anger/Sadness/Fear/Shame) ___

INSIGHT: Failed attempts = proof the desire exists AND is unsatisfied.
Every complaint is an unsatisfied desire crying out.
```

**Step 3: Follow the Fears**

```
What keeps them up at 3AM?
- [ ] List 3 fears from VOC (with quotes): ___
- [ ] What is the WORST outcome they imagine? ___
- [ ] What are they afraid others will think/notice? ___
- [ ] What do they fear about the FUTURE? ___

INSIGHT: Fear is the most powerful emotional fuel for desire.
The desire to ESCAPE fear is often stronger than the desire to GAIN.
```

**Step 4: Follow the Envy**

```
Who do they wish they could be?
- [ ] Who in their world has what they want? ___
- [ ] What specific thing about that person triggers envy? ___
- [ ] What would they need to feel EQUAL to that person? ___

INSIGHT: Envy reveals the hidden desire. They won't say it aloud,
but envy shows you EXACTLY what they want.
```

**Step 5: Map the Three Desire Levels**

```
PRIMARY DESIRE (stated openly):
"I want to ____________________________________"

SECONDARY DESIRE (consequence of achieving primary):
"So that I can _________________________________"

HIDDEN DESIRE (won't admit, must be implied):
"Because deep down I ____________________________"
```

**Step 6: Validate with VOC**

```
For each desire level, find AT LEAST 3 VOC quotes that confirm it:

PRIMARY:
1. "___" (Source: ___)
2. "___" (Source: ___)
3. "___" (Source: ___)

SECONDARY:
1. "___" (Source: ___)
2. "___" (Source: ___)
3. "___" (Source: ___)

HIDDEN (inferred from context, not direct quotes):
1. Implied by: "___" (Source: ___)
2. Implied by: "___" (Source: ___)
3. Implied by: "___" (Source: ___)
```

## Part 6: Intensification Methods (5 Techniques)

When the market has seen every claim, you do NOT make new claims.
You make the EXISTING claim more vivid, more specific, more real.
Here are 5 techniques with fill-in templates.

### Technique 1: MAKE IT VIVID

Transform abstract benefits into concrete sensory experiences.

```
BEFORE (abstract): "Feel better"
PROCESS: What does "better" look, feel, sound like in their daily life?
AFTER (vivid): "Wake up without that 3AM chest tightness for the first time in years"

Template:
"[Specific sensory experience] for the first time in [timeframe]"

Fill-in exercise:
Abstract benefit: ___
What it looks like: ___
What it feels like: ___
What it sounds like: ___
When it happens: ___
VIVID version: ___
```

### Technique 2: MAKE IT IMMEDIATE

Collapse the timeline. Take a future benefit and make it feel like it starts NOW.

```
BEFORE (vague future): "You'll see results"
PROCESS: When is the FIRST noticeable change? Be specific.
AFTER (immediate): "In 72 hours, you'll notice the first change"

Template:
"In [specific short timeframe], you'll [first noticeable change]"

Fill-in exercise:
Vague benefit: ___
First possible change point: ___
What they'll notice first: ___
IMMEDIATE version: ___
```

### Technique 3: MAKE IT PERSONAL

Replace generic pronouns with specific, personal language that makes
the reader feel the copy is written FOR THEM.

```
BEFORE (generic): "People lose weight with this method"
PROCESS: YOUR body. YOUR mirror. YOUR specific situation.
AFTER (personal): "YOUR belly. YOUR jeans. YOUR reflection at 7AM tomorrow morning."

Template:
"YOUR [specific thing] + YOUR [specific situation] + YOUR [specific moment]"

Fill-in exercise:
Generic version: ___
Their specific body part/situation: ___
Their specific moment of awareness: ___
PERSONAL version: ___
```

### Technique 4: MAKE IT CONSEQUENTIAL

Show what happens if they do NOT act. Make the cost of inaction visceral.

```
BEFORE (low stakes): "Try our solution"
PROCESS: What happens in 6 months without action? 1 year? 5 years?
AFTER (consequential): "What happens to your marriage if the ringing keeps getting louder? What happens when you can't hear your grandchild's voice?"

Template:
"What happens to your [relationship/health/career] if [problem continues]?
What happens when [worst realistic consequence]?"

Fill-in exercise:
Current problem: ___
6-month consequence: ___
1-year consequence: ___
Relational consequence: ___
CONSEQUENTIAL version: ___
```

### Technique 5: MAKE IT INEVITABLE

Remove doubt. Make the outcome feel like a statistical certainty, not a gamble.

```
BEFORE (uncertain): "You might see results"
PROCESS: What's the data? What percentage? What's the track record?
AFTER (inevitable): "87.3% of participants reported measurable improvement within 21 days. Not might. Not maybe. 87.3%."

Template:
"[Specific percentage] of [specific population] [reported/achieved] [specific result]
within [specific timeframe]. Not might. Not maybe. [Repeat number]."

Fill-in exercise:
Uncertain version: ___
Best available data point: ___
Specific population: ___
Specific result: ___
INEVITABLE version: ___
```

### Stacking All 5 Techniques

The real power comes from STACKING multiple techniques on the
same core promise:

```
Core promise: "Helps with tinnitus"

+ VIVID: "Silences the high-pitched ringing that wakes you at 3AM"
+ IMMEDIATE: "First reduction noticeable in 72 hours"
+ PERSONAL: "YOUR ears. YOUR sleep. YOUR sanity."
+ CONSEQUENTIAL: "Before the ringing gets loud enough to drown out conversations with people you love"
+ INEVITABLE: "87.3% of 847 European participants reported significant improvement within 21 days"

STACKED HEADLINE:
"The 'Otolith Crystal' formula that silenced the 3AM ringing
for 87.3% of European participants in 21 days -- before the
damage becomes irreversible"
```

## Part 7: Quick Reference Card

```
=== AWARENESS ===
1 = Unaware      → Story/Curiosity (no product, no problem)
2 = Problem       → Symptom + Empathy (validate their pain)
3 = Solution      → Mechanism (why YOUR approach is different)
4 = Product       → Proof (overcome their specific objection)
5 = Most Aware    → Deal (get out of the way and let them buy)

=== SOPHISTICATION ===
1 = First         → Direct claim ("Lose weight!")
2 = Second        → Bigger claim ("Lose 30 lbs in 30 days!")
3 = Third         → Mechanism ("The Keto Reset that melts 30 lbs")
4 = Fourth        → Intensified mechanism + proof + authority
5 = Fifth         → Story + Identification (zero direct claims)

=== 3 CARDINAL RULES ===
RULE 1: Diagnose BEFORE writing. Never write a single word
        without knowing awareness + sophistication level.
RULE 2: Write for ONE level LOWER than you think. Better to
        over-educate than to lose them with assumptions.
RULE 3: At sophistication 3+, MECHANISM is the headline.
        At sophistication 5, STORY beats every claim.

=== MASS DESIRE ===
PRIMARY:   What they openly want       → Headline
SECONDARY: Consequence of getting it   → Body copy
HIDDEN:    What they won't admit       → Implied, never stated

=== PROOF HIERARCHY ===
Demonstration > Documentation > Credentials > Testimonials > Claims
ALWAYS stack. Never rely on claims alone.

=== MOST COMMON DR POSITIONS ===
- Problem Aware + Soph 3-4: Lead with mechanism (MOST offers)
- Solution Aware + Soph 3: Sweet spot for new product launches
- Unaware + Soph 5: Hardest cell. Pure storytelling required.
```

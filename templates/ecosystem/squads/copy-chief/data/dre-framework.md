# DRE Framework — Dominant Resident Emotion

## Definition
The DRE is the dominant emotional state that the avatar carries about their problem. It's not what they THINK about the problem — it's what they FEEL. The DRE is identified through VOC analysis, never assumed.

## 5 Escalation Levels

### Level 1: Surface
Generic awareness of the problem.
"I have [problem]."
Copy at this level: informational, educational, low urgency.

### Level 2: Personal
Problem affects their daily life specifically.
"This happens to ME every day when I [specific routine]."
Copy at this level: relatable, specific, uses sinestesia da rotina.

### Level 3: Relational
Problem impacts relationships with others.
"My [partner/family/colleagues] notice. They [specific behavior]."
Copy at this level: social proof of suffering, relationship impact.

### Level 4: Identity
Problem threatens who they are or who they're becoming.
"Am I becoming [feared identity]? Is this who I am now?"
Copy at this level: identity crisis, deep fear, transformation urgency.

### Level 5: Existential
Deepest fear — permanent damage, irreversibility, loss.
"What if it's too late? What if this never gets better?"
Copy at this level: urgency to act NOW, consequences of inaction.

## Application in Copy

| Element | DRE Entry | DRE Exit | Target Level |
|---------|-----------|----------|-------------|
| Hook/Creative | 1-2 | 2-3 | Interrupt + Curiosity |
| Lead (VSL first 2 min) | 2-3 | 3-4 | Identification + Desire |
| Body (VSL middle) | 3-4 | 4-5 | Belief + Urgency |
| Close/CTA | 4-5 | 5 (action) | Decision + Action |
| Subject Line (email) | 3-4 | — | Open rate driver |

## Validation
- `emotional_stress_test` genericidade >= 8 confirms DRE activation
- `blind_critic` checks DRE escalation per section
- VOC quotes at intensity 4-5 must support identified DRE

## Anti-Patterns
- DRE assumed without VOC evidence
- Copy stays at level 1-2 throughout (comfortable = failed)
- DRE mentioned but not FELT (describing emotion vs activating it)
- Generic emotion ("feel better") instead of specific ("stop waking up at 3am terrified")
